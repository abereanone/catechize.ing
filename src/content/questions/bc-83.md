The ninth commandment forbiddeth whatsoever is prejudicial to the truth, or injurious to our own or our neighbour’s good name.

## Proofs
- The ninth commandment forbiddeth whatsoever is prejudicial to the truth, or injurious to our own or our neighbour’s good name. (1 Samuel 17:28; Leviticus 19:16; Psalm 15:2–3)

## Additional Exposition

### Section 1

**Q:** Is lying a great sin?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Lying lips are an abomination to the Lord, (Proverbs 12:22)

**Q:** Is it a sin that makes us like the devil?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For when he speaketh a lie he speaketh of his own, (John 8:44)

**Q:** Is it an unprofitable sin?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** The getting of treasures by a lying tongue is vanity tossed to and fro of them that seek death, (Proverbs 21:6)

**Q:** Is it a sin that will be detected?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For a lying tongue is but fora moment, (Proverbs 12:19)

**Q:** And severely punished?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For without are dogs, and whoso loveth and maketh a lie, (Revelation 22:15)

**Q:** Should we therefore hate it?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** I abhor lying, (Psalm 119:163)

**Q:** And pray against it?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Remove from me the way of lying, (Psalm 119:29)

### Section 2

**Q:** May we tell lies to make sport?  
**A:** No.  
**Proof:** They make the king glad with their wickedness, and the princes with their lies, (Hosea 7:3)

**Q:** Or to excuse a fault?  
**A:** No.  
**Proof:** As Gehazi, who said, thy servant went no whither, (2 Kings 5:25)

**Q:** Or with an intention to do good?  
**A:** No.  
**Proof:** We must not do evil that good may come, (Romans 3:8)

### Section 3

**Q:** Is detraction and slander a sin?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Thou sittest and slanderest thine own mother’s (Psalm 50:20)

**Q:** But may we not return slander for slander?  
**A:** No.  
**Proof:** Render not railing for railing, (1 Peter 3:9)

**Q:** Is flattery also a sin?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** They speak vanity every one with his neighbour, with flattering lips and a double heart do they speak, (Psalm 12:2)

**Q:** Is self detraction a sin?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** There is that maketh himself poor and yet hath great riches, (Proverbs 13:7)

**Q:** And self-flattery?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** There is that maketh himself rich, and yet hath nothing, (Proverbs 13:7)

### Section 4

**Q:** Doth this commandment forbid perjury?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** I will be a swift witness against false swearers, (Malachi 3:5)

**Q:** And subornation?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Thus the Jews set up false witnesses against Stephen, (Acts 6:11, 13)

**Q:** And backbiting and talebearing?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Thou shalt not go up and down as a talebearer among thy people, (Leviticus 19:16)

**Q:** And all censorious and uncharitable reflections?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For he that speaketh evil of his brother, and judgeth his brother, speaks evil of the law, and judgeth the law, (James 4:11)
