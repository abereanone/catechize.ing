Our first parents being left to the freedom of their own will, fell from the estate wherein they were created, by sinning against God.

## Proofs
- Our first parents being left to the freedom of their own will, fell from the estate wherein they were created, by sinning against God. (Genesis 3:6–8, 13; Ecclesiastes 7:29)

## Additional Exposition

### Section 1

**Q:** Did man fall from that estate in which he was created?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** They have sought out many inventions, (Ecclesiastes 7:29)

**Q:** Did he fall from a state of knowledge?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** His understanding is darkened, (Ephesians 4:18)

**Q:** Did he fall from a state of holiness?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** He is alienated from the life of God, (Ephesians 4:18)

**Q:** And from a state of freedom?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** He that committeth sin is the servant of sin, (John 8:34)

**Q:** And from a state of rest?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** There is no peace, saith my God, to the wicked, (Isaiah 57:21)

**Q:** And from a state of communion with God?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For Adam and his wife hid themselves from the presence of the Lord God, (Genesis 3:8)

**Q:** And from a state of happiness?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** So he drove out the man, (Genesis 3:24)

**Q:** And do all mankind imitate the sin of their first parents?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** They, like Adam, have transgressed the covenant, (Hosea 6:7)

### Section 2

**Q:** Did God foresee Adam’s sin?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** I knew that thou wouldest deal very treacherously, (Isaiah 48:8)

**Q:** But was he the author of it?  
**A:** No.  
**Proof:** For he is a God of truth and without iniquity, just and right is he, (Deuteronomy 32:4)

**Q:** Is man’s ruin then from himself?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** O Israel, thou hast destroyed thyself, (Hosea 13:9)

### Practical response

**Q:** Should Adam’s fall teach us to be watchful over ourselves?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Let him that thinketh he standeth take heed lest he fall, (1 Corinthians 10:12)

**Q:** And to disclaim all confidence in the creature?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For surely men of low degree are vanity, and men of high degree are a lie, (Psalm 62:9)
