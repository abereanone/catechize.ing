The ninth commandment requireth the maintaining and promoting of truth between man and man, and of our own and our neighbour’s good name, especially in witness bearing.

## Proofs
- The ninth commandment requireth the maintaining and promoting of truth between man and man, (Zechariah 8:16)
- and of our own and our neighbour’s good name, (John 5:12)
- especially in witness bearing. (Proverbs 14:5, 25)

## Additional Exposition

### Practical response

**Q:** Should we endeavour to get a good name?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** If there be any virtue, if there be any praise, think on these things, (Philippians 4:8)

**Q:** And to keep it?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For a little folly disgraceth him that is in reputation for wisdom and honour, (Ecclesiastes 10:1)

**Q:** May we for this purpose vindicate our character when aspersed?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** My righteousness I hold fast, and will not let it go. (Job 27:6)

**Q:** And speak modestly in our own commendation?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** In nothing am I behind the very chiefest apostles, though I am nothing, (2 Corinthians 12:11)

### Section 2

**Q:** Is a good name an honour to God?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** On your part he is glorified, (1 Peter 4:14)

**Q:** And a credit to religion?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** We will go with you, for we have heard that God is with you, (Zechariah 8:23)

**Q:** And a comfort to ourselves?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For a good report maketh the bones fat, (Proverbs 15:30)

**Q:** But do all enjoy this blessing who deserve it?  
**A:** No.  
**Proof:** For I heard the defaming of many, (Jeremiah 20:10)

### Practical response

**Q:** Should we be tender of others' good name as well as our own?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** We also bear record, (3 John 12)

**Q:** Should we for this purpose cover their faults?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** He that covereth transgression seeketh love, (Proverbs 17:9)

**Q:** And be secret in our reproofs?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Tell him between him and thee alone, (Matthew 18:15)

**Q:** Should we put the best construction on their actions?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Charity thinketh no evil, (1 Corinthians 13:5)

**Q:** And discourage backbiting and detraction?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** We must with an angry countenance drive away a backbiting tongue, (Proverbs 25:23)

**Q:** Should we also vindicate them from unjust aspersions?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Let not the king sin against David, because his works have been to thee-ward very good, (1 Samuel 19:4)

**Q:** And joyfully receive all reports to their advantage?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** I rejoiced greatly when the brethren came and testified of the truth that is in thee, (3 John 3)

**Q:** And will a regard to others' good name be the best security to our own?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For with what measure you mete, it shall be measured to you again, (Matthew 7:2)

### Section 4

**Q:** Is it our duty to speak the truth?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Speak ye every man the truth to his neighbour, (Zechariah 8:16)

**Q:** And when called thereto the whole truth?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Samuel told Eli every whit, and hid nothing from him, (1 Samuel 3:18)

**Q:** And nothing but the truth?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Surely they are my people, children that will not lie, (Isaiah 63:8)

**Q:** Should truth be observed in our common conversation?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Lie not one to another, (Colossians 3:9)

**Q:** And in our promise and engagements?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Better it is that thou shouldst not vow, than vow and not pay, (Ecclesiastes 5:5)

**Q:** But especially in witness-bearing?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For a true witness delivereth souls, (Proverbs 14:25)

### Section 5

**Q:** In order to speak the truth should we think before we speak?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Whatsoever things are true, think on these things, (Philippians 4:8)

**Q:** And accustom ourselves to a sober way of speaking?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** I speak the words of truth and soberness, (Acts 26:25)

**Q:** And be careful of rash promises?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Suffer not thy mouth to cause thy flesh to sin, neither say thou before the angel, it was an error, (Ecclesiastes 5:6)

**Q:** And endeavour to overcome the fear of man?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Whom hast thou feared that thou hast lied? (Isaiah 57:11)

**Q:** And not do any thing that we should be ashamed to own?  
**A:** No.  
**Proof:** As Sarah, who first laughed, and then denied it, saying, I laughed not, (Genesis 18:15)
