God having out of his mere good pleasure, from all eternity, elected some to everlasting life, did enter into a covenant of grace, to deliver them out of the estate of sin and misery, and to bring them into an estate of salvation by a Redeemer.

## Proofs
- God having out of his mere good pleasure, from all eternity, elected some to everlasting life, (Ephesians 1:4–5)
- did enter into a covenant of grace, to deliver them out of the estate of sin and misery, and to bring them into an estate of salvation by a Redeemer. (Romans 3:20–22; Galatians 3:21–22)

## Additional Exposition

### Section 1

**Q:** Shall any of the human race be recovered from the ruins of the fall?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** A remnant shall be saved, (Romans 9:27)

**Q:** Are all others left to perish in their sins?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** The rest were blinded, (Romans 11:7)

**Q:** But is God’s decree the impulsive cause of their ruin?  
**A:** No.  
**Proof:** For when lust hath conceived, it bringeth forth sin, and sin when it is finished bringeth forth death, (James 1:15)

### Section 2

**Q:** Are all the saints the objects of God’s eternal choice?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** God hath from the beginning chosen you, (2 Thessalonians 2:13)

**Q:** And are they chosen to happiness as the end?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For God hath not appointed us to wrath, but to obtain salvation, (1 Thessalonians 5:9)

**Q:** And to sanctification as the means?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Ye are chosen to salvation through sanctification, (2 Thessalonians 2:13)

**Q:** Are they chosen in Christ?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** According as he hath chosen us in him, (Ephesians 1:4)

**Q:** And is it a certain number that is thus chosen?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** The Lord knoweth them that are his, (2 Timothy 2:19)

### Section 3

**Q:** Doth the decree of election extend but to a few comparatively?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Few are chosen, (Matthew 20:16)

**Q:** But does it extend to some of all nations?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Thou hast redeemed us to God out of every kindred, and tongue, and people, and nation, (Revelation 5:9)

**Q:** And to some of the chief of sinners?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Paul was a chosen vessel, yet the chief of sinners, (Acts 9:15)

**Q:** And to all that are willing to come to Christ?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Him that cometh to me, I will in no wise cast out, (John 6:37)

**Q:** Is this then an encouragement to use the means?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Strive to enter in at the strait gate, (Luke 13:24)

### Section 4

**Q:** Is the decree of election founded upon God’s sovereign pleasure?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** I will have mercy upon whom I will have mercy, (Romans 9:15)

**Q:** And upon nothing in the creature?  
**A:** No.  
**Proof:** Ye have not chosen me, but I have chosen you, (John 15:16)

**Q:** Is it secret?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** It is called the mystery of his will, (Ephesians 1:9)

**Q:** And firm and irrevocable?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** The foundation of God standeth sure, (2 Timothy 2:19)

### Section 5

**Q:** Doth God exercise all special grace through Christ?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** We are blessed with all spiritual blessings in Christ, (Ephesians 1:3)

**Q:** Were there mutual engagements for this purpose between the Father and Son?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** The counsel of peace shall be between them both, (Zechariah 6:13)

**Q:** Were all the promises of the covenant primarily made to Christ?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Hence they are called the sure mercies of David, (Isaiah 55:3)

**Q:** Were all the conditions of it exacted from him?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** The Lord hath laid on him the iniquity of us all, (Isaiah 53:6)

**Q:** Did Christ freely undertake the work of our redemption?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Lo, I come to do thy will, O God, (Hebrews 10:7)

**Q:** And did the Father engage for the success of his undertaking?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** He shall see the travail of his soul and be satisfied, (Isaiah 53:11)

### Section 6

**Q:** Are the saints in covenant given to Christ?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Thine they were, and thou gavest them me, (John 17:6)

**Q:** Are they given to him to be redeemed?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** To redeem them that were under the law, (Galatians 4:5)

**Q:** And to be called?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Them I must bring, (John 10:16)

**Q:** And to be preserved?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Those that thou gavest me I have kept, (John 17:12)

**Q:** And to be finally glorified?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Thou hast given him power over all flesh, that he should give eternal life to as many as thou hast given him, (John 17:2)

**Q:** And shall all this be certainly accomplished?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** The pleasure of the Lord shall prosper in his hand, (Isaiah 53:10)

### Section 7

**Q:** Could we be saved by the first covenant?  
**A:** No.  
**Proof:** For if there had been a law which could have given life, verily righteousness should have been by the law, (Galatians 5:21)

**Q:** Is the new covenant then a great privilege?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** It is good tidings of great joy, (Luke 2:10)

**Q:** And should we embrace it as such?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** It should be all our salvation, and all our desire, (2 Samuel 23:5)
