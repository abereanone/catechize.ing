## Proofs

## Additional Exposition

### Section 1

**Q:** Is piety towards God a necessary branch of true religion?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Add to patience, goodness, (2 Peter 1:6)

**Q:** And is righteousness towards men a branch of the same?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Follow that which is good to all men, (1 Thessalonians 5:15)

**Q:** Doth the law of God require both?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** To do justly, to love mercy, and to walk humbly with God, Mich. vi.£. Doth the grace of the gospel teach both? Yes.*′ To live soberly, righteously, and godly, (Titus 3:12)

**Q:** Have good men practiced both?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Herein do I exercise myself, to have always a conscience void of offence toward God and toward men, (Acts 24:16)

**Q:** Ought these two therefore never to be separated?  
**A:** No.  
**Proof:** For what God hath joined together let no man put asunder, (Matthew 19:6)

### Section 2

**Q:** Are kings and supreme governors included under the general title of fathers?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Blessed be the kingdom of our father David, (Mark 11:10)

**Q:** And pastors of churches?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Ye have not many fathers, for in Christ Jesus I have begotten you through the gospel, (1 Corinthians 4:15)

**Q:** And masters of families?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Naaman’s servants said unto him, my Father, (2 Kings 5:3)

**Q:** And all superiors either in age and office, or in gifts and endowments?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Hear, ye children, the instruction of a father, (Proverbs 4:1)

**Q:** And is proper respect due to all these according to their different characters and stations?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Render therefore honour to whom honour is due, (Romans 13:7)
