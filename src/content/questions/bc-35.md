They that are effectually called do in this life partake of justification, adoption, sanctification, and the several benefits which in this life do either accompany or flow from them.

## Proofs
- They that are effectually called do in this life partake of justification, (Romans 8:30)
- adoption, (Ephesians 1:5)
- sanctification, and the several benefits which in this life do either accompany or flow from them. (1 Corinthians 1:30)

## Additional Exposition

### Section 1

**Q:** Is effectual calling of great advantage?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Godliness is profitable unto all things, (1 Timothy 4:8)

**Q:** Is it of immediate advantage?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** If ye be willing and obedient ye shall eat the good of the land, (Isaiah 1:19)

**Q:** And of eternal advantage?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For it hath the promise not only of the life that now is, but of that which is to come, (1 Timothy 4:8)

**Q:** Doth it bring into a state of grace?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Such are not under the law, but under grace, (Romans 6:15)

**Q:** And into a state of comfort?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** We who have believed do enter into rest, (Hebrews 4:3)

**Q:** And into a state of holiness?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** God hath not called us to uncleanness, but to holiness, (1 Thessalonians 4:7)

**Q:** And is it an earnest of everlasting glory?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For God will give grace and glory, and no good thing will he withhold from those who walk uprightly, (Psalm 84:11)

### Section 2

**Q:** Are those who are effectually called justified?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Whom he called, them he also justified, (Romans 8:30)

**Q:** Is this a great privilege?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Blessed is the man to whom the Lord imputeth not iniquity, (Psalm 32:2)

**Q:** Is it the certain and enduring privilege of all the saints?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** There is no condemnation to them that are in Christ Jesus, (Romans 8:1)

**Q:** And are all others excluded?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** They are in the gall of bitterness and in the bond of iniquity, (Acts 8:23)

### Section 3

**Q:** Are all those that are effectually called adopted?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Ya are all the children of God by faith in Jesus Christ, (Galatians 3:26)

**Q:** Is this a great instance of love in God?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Behold what manner of love the Father hath bestowed upon us, that we should be called the sons of God, (1 John 3:1)

**Q:** And a great blessing to men?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For if children, then heirs, (Romans 8:17)

**Q:** Are such also sanctified?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** They are sanctified in Christ Jesus, (1 Corinthians 1:2)

**Q:** And without this would their happiness be incomplete?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For without holiness none shall see the Lord, (Hebrews 12:14)

### Section 4

**Q:** Have those who are effectually called a title to all other covenant blessings?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** All things are yours, (1 Corinthians 3:22)

**Q:** And may they take the comfort of them?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** As having nothing and yet possessing all things, (2 Corinthians 6:10)

**Q:** And is this their case without exception?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For the same Lord over all is rich unto all that call upon him, (Romans 10:12)
