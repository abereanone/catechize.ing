Every sin deserveth God’s wrath and curse, both in this life and that which is to come.

## Proofs
- Every sin deserveth God’s wrath and curse, both in this life and that which is to come. (Ephesians 5:6; Galatians 3:10; Lamentations 3:39; Matthew 25:41; Romans 6:23)

## Additional Exposition

### Section 1

**Q:** Is sin against the body of man?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** There is no rest in my bones because of my sin, (Psalm 38:3)

**Q:** And against the soul of man?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** He that sinneth against me wrongeth his own soul, (Proverbs 8:36)

**Q:** Is it against his temporal interest?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** If ye will walk contrary to me, then I will walk contrary to you, and will bring a sword upon you, (Leviticus 26:24–25)

**Q:** And against his eternal welfare?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Depart from me, ye that work iniquity, (Matthew 7:23)

**Q:** And are all sins, though not alike heinous, alike destructive?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For he that said, do not commit adultery, said also, do not kill, (James 2:11)

### Section 2

**Q:** Doth sin subject to the wrath of God?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Behold thou art wroth, for we have sinned, (Isaiah 64:6)

**Q:** Is the wrath of a common man dreadful?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Cursed be their wrath, for it was cruel, (Genesis 49:7)

**Q:** But may this wrath be appeased?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** A soft answer turneth away wrath, (Proverbs 15:1)

**Q:** Is the wrath of a king more dreadful?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** The king’s wrath is as the roaring of a lion, (Proverbs 19:12)

**Q:** But may this wrath be pacified?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Then was the king’s wrath pacified, (Esther 7:10)

**Q:** Is the wrath of devils more terrible than either?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Woe to the inhabitants of the earth, for the devil is come down, having great wrath, (Revelation 12:12)

**Q:** But may this wrath be restrained?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** He bound Satan a thousand years, (Revelation 20:2)

**Q:** Is God’s wrath then infinitely more dreadful than all?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Who knoweth the power of thine anger? (Psalm 90:11)

### Section 3

**Q:** Doth sin expose men to the curse?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Ye are cursed with a curse, (Malachi 3:9)

**Q:** Will this curse certainly come upon impenitent sinners?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** All these curses shall come upon thee and overtake thee, (Deuteronomy 28:15)

**Q:** Are they cursed whilst they live?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Their portion is cursed in the earth, (Job 24:18)

**Q:** And cursed in death?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Ye shall leave your name for a curse to my chosen, for the Lord shall slay thee, (Isaiah 65:15)

**Q:** And cursed for ever?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** They are nigh unto cursing whose end is to be burned, (Hebrews 6:8)

### Section 4

**Q:** Is the wrath and curse of God the due desert of sin?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Whose damnation is just, (Romans 3:8)

**Q:** And of every sin?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Cursed is every one that continueth not in all things that are written in the book of the law to do them, (Galatians 3:10)

**Q:** But is there not a possibility of deliverance herefrom?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For God hath devised means that his banished soul should not be expelled from him, (2 Samuel 14:14)
