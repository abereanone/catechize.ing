The third commandment is, Thou shalt not take the name of the Lord thy God in vain; for the Lord will not hold him guiltless that taketh his name in vain.

## Proofs
- The third commandment is, Thou shalt not take the name of the Lord thy God in vain; for the Lord will not hold him guiltless that taketh his name in vain. (Exodus 20:7)

## Additional Exposition

### Section 1

**Q:** By God’s name are we to understand his nature?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** My name is in him, (speaking of Christ) (Exodus 23:21)

**Q:** And his essential properties?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** I will make all my goodness to pass before thee, and I will proclaim the name of the Lord, (Exodus 33:19)

**Q:** And his titles?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** If they shall say, what is his name? What shall I answer? And God said, I am that I am, (Exodus 3:13–14)

**Q:** And any thing whereby he maketh himself known?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** I have manifested thy name unto the men thou gavest me, (John 17:6)

### Practical response

**Q:** Should we reverence the name of God?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** That thou mayest fear this glorious and fearful name, the Lord thy God, (Deuteronomy 28:58)

**Q:** And trust in it?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** The name of the Lord is a strong tower, the righteous runneth into it and is safe, (Proverbs 18:10)

**Q:** And walk in it?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** We will walk in the name of the Lord our God, for ever and for ever, (Micah 4:5)

**Q:** And should we do this by no other means?  
**A:** No.  
**Proof:** I will take away the names of Baalim out of her mouth, (Hosea 2:17)

### Section 3

**Q:** Hath God a great regard for his own name?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For how should my name be polluted, (Isaiah 48:11)

**Q:** Is it therefore a great sin for us to be careless of it?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Neither shalt thou profane the name of thy God, I am the Lord, (Leviticus 18:21)
