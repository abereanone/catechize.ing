The sixth commandment absolutely forbiddeth the taking away of our own life, or the life of our neighbour unjustly, or whatsoever tendeth thereunto.

## Proofs
- The sixth commandment absolutely forbiddeth the taking away of our own life, or the life of our neighbour unjustly, or whatsoever tendeth thereunto. (Acts 26:28; Genesis 9:9)

## Additional Exposition

### Section 1

**Q:** Is it a sin to destroy ourselves?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** shouldest thou die before thy time? (Ecclesiastes 7:17)

**Q:** And of envy?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Envy is the rottenness of the bones, (Proverbs 14:30)

**Q:** And of passion?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** I do well to be angry, even unto death, (Jonah 4:9)

**Q:** And of uncleanness?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** He that committeth fornication sinneth Against his own body, (1 Corinthians 6:18)

**Q:** And of intemperance?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** At last it biteth like a serpent, and stingeth like an adder, (Proverbs 23:32)

**Q:** And of immoderate care and sorrow?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For the sorrow of the world worketh death, (2 Corinthians 7:10)

### Section 2

**Q:** Is this an encroachment upon God’s prerogative?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For ’tis he that killeth and maketh alive, (Deuteronomy 32:39)

**Q:** And a compliance with Satan’s temptations?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For he was a murderer from the beginning, (John 8:44)

**Q:** But may we not expose our lives for the sake of a good conscience?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** They loved not their lives unto death, (Revelation 12:11)

**Q:** And hazard them in a good cause?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Zebulon and Naphtali jeoparded their lives in the high places of the field, (Judges 5:18)

### Section 3

**Q:** Is it a breach of this command for magistrates to take away the lives of capital offenders?  
**A:** No.  
**Proof:** Thou shalt do according to the sentence ; and the man that will do presumptuously, even that man shall die, (Deuteronomy 17:10, 12)

**Q:** Or for one man to kill another in lawful war?  
**A:** No.  
**Proof:** Smite Amalek, and slay both man and woman, infant and suckling, (1 Samuel 15:3)

**Q:** Or by accident?  
**A:** No.  
**Proof:** Whoso killeth his neighbour ignorantly, whom he hateth not in time past, shall flee to the city of refuge, (Deuteronomy 19:4–5)

**Q:** Or in his own defence?  
**A:** No.  
**Proof:** For if a thief be found breaking up, and be smitten that he die, then shall no blood be shed for him, (Exodus 22:2)

### Section 4

**Q:** But is wilful murder a great sin?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** It was said by them of old time, thou shalt not kill, (Matthew 5:21)

**Q:** Is it a crying sin?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** The voice of thy brother’s blood crieth to me from the ground, (Genesis 4:10)

**Q:** Is it a sin that calls for present punishment?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Thou shalt take him from my altar that he may die, (Exodus 21:14)

**Q:** And exposes to future damnation?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For no murderer hath eternal life, (1 John 3:15)

**Q:** Ought we therefore to pray that God would keep us from it?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Deliver me from blood-guiltiness, O God, (Psalm 51:14)

### Section 5

**Q:** Is it a breach of this commandment to counsel the death of others?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Thou hast slain him with the sword of the children of Ammon, (2 Samuel 12:9)

**Q:** Or to consent to it?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Ye by wicked hands have crucified and slain, (Acts 2:23)

**Q:** Or to be any way accessary to it?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** I have sinned, in that I have betrayed innocent blood, (Matthew 27:4)

**Q:** Or to rejoice at it?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** If I have rejoiced at the destruction of him that hated me, (Job 31:29)

### Section 6

**Q:** Is unjust and immoderate anger a breach of this commandment?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Whoso is angry with his brother without a case, is in danger of the judgment, (Matthew 5:22)

**Q:** And foul and provoking language?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Whosoever shall say unto his brother, Racha, or thou fool, shall be in danger of hell fire, (Matthew 5:22)

**Q:** And malice and hatred?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** He that hateth his brother is a murderer, (1 John 3:15)

**Q:** And is there such a thing as soul murder?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** If thou givest him not warning his blood will I require at thine hand, (Ezekiel 3:18)
