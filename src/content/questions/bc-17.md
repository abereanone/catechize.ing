Sin is any want of conformity unto, or transgression of, the law of God.

## Proofs
- Sin is any want of conformity unto, or transgression of, the law of God. (1 John 3:4)

## Additional Exposition

### Section 1

**Q:** Doth sin suppose a law?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For where no law is there is no transgression, (Romans 4:15)

**Q:** Is sin the breach of law?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For it is the transgression of the law, (1 John 3:4)

**Q:** Is the omission of what the law requires sin?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** They hearkened not unto my words nor to my law, (Jeremiah 6:19)

**Q:** And the commission of what it forbids?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** They have trespassed against my law, (Hosea 8:1)

**Q:** Is sin then aggravated by a knowledge of God’s law?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** And knowest his will, being instructed out of the law, (Romans 2:18)

### Section 2

**Q:** Doth the law of God extend to our thoughts?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** He that looketh upon a woman to lust after her committeth adultery in his heart, (Matthew 5:28)

**Q:** And to our words?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Let no corrupt communication proceed out of your mouth, (Ephesians 4:29)

**Q:** And to our actions?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** O do not this abominable thing which I hate, (Jeremiah 44:4)

**Q:** And to the temper of our minds?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Thou shalt love the Lord thy God with all thine heart, and with all thy soul, and with all thy mind, (Matthew 22:37)

**Q:** And is every breach of the law sinful?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For all unrighteousness is sin, (1 John 5:17)
