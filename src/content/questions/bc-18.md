The sin whereby our first parents fell from the estate wherein they were created, was their eating the forbidden fruit.

## Proofs
- The sin whereby our first parents fell from the estate wherein they were created, was their eating the forbidden fruit. (Genesis 3:6, 12, 16–17)

## Additional Exposition

<a href="https://baptistcatechism.org/beddome/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">From Benjamin Beddome</a>


### Section 1

**Q:** Did our first parents sin?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Thy first father hath sinned, (Isaiah 43:27)

**Q:** Did they sin by eating the forbidden fruit?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For she took of the fruit and did eat, and gave also to her husband with her, and he did eat, (Genesis 3:6)

**Q:** Was there in this sin the lust of the flesh?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For she saw that the tree was good for food, (Genesis 3:6)

**Q:** And the lust of the eye?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For she saw that it was pleasant to the eyes, (Genesis 3:6)

**Q:** And the pride of life?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For she saw that it was a tree to be desired to make one wise, (Genesis 3:6)

**Q:** Was it an instance of horrid rebellion against God?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Hast thou eaten of the tree whereof I commanded thee that thou shouldest not eat, (Genesis 3:11)

**Q:** And of amazing folly in man?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** This their way was their folly, (Psalm 49:13)

### Section 2

**Q:** Did our first parents fall without a tempter?  
**A:** No.  
**Proof:** For the serpent said unto the woman, Yea, hath God said, (Genesis 3:1)

**Q:** Was this serpent the devil?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** The old serpent, which is the devil, and Satan, (Revelation 20:2)

**Q:** But could he force their consent?  
**A:** No.  
**Proof:** Resist the devil and he will fly from you, (James 4:7)

**Q:** Did he notwithstanding prevail?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** The serpent beguiled me and I did eat, (Genesis 3:13)

**Q:** Did he in all this aim at their destruction?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For he is Abaddon, (the destroyer), (Revelation 9:11)

### Section 3

**Q:** Did Satan act a subtil part in tempting?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** The serpent beguiled Eve through his subtilty, (2 Corinthians 11:3)

**Q:** Did he promise impunity?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Ye shall not surely die, (Genesis 3:4)

**Q:** Did he promise improvement?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Your eyes shall be opened, (Genesis 3:5)

**Q:** Did he feed them with high thoughts of themselves?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Ye shall be as gods, (Genesis 3:5)

**Q:** Did he suggest to them hard thoughts of God?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For he said, God doth know this, (Genesis 3:5)

**Q:** Was it an instance of his policy first to assault the woman?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For she is the weaker vessel, (1 Peter 3:7)

**Q:** And to assault her when alone?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For two are better than one—for if they fall, the one will lift up the his fellow, (Ecclesiastes 4:9–10)
