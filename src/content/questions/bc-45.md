The rule which God at first revealed to man for his obedience, was the moral law.

## Proofs
- The rule which God at first revealed to man for his obedience, was the moral law. (Romans 2:14–15; 10:5)

## Additional Exposition

### Section 1

**Q:** Were there positive institutions under the old testament?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** There were meats, and drinks, and diverse washings and carnal ordinances, (Hebrews 9:10)

**Q:** Were these suited to the infant state of the church?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Even so we, when we were children, were in bondage under the elements of the world, (Galatians 4:3)

**Q:** But are they now abolished?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For they were only imposed till the time of reformation, (Hebrews 9:10)

**Q:** Is it our great mercy to be free from them?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For they were a yoke which neither our fathers nor we were able to bear, (Acts 15:10)

**Q:** Do we owe this liberty to Christ?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For the priesthood being changed, there is made of necessity a change also of the law, (Hebrews 8:12)

**Q:** And should we stand fast in it?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Be not entangled again with the yoke of bondage, (Galatians 5:1)

### Section 2

**Q:** Are there positive institutions under the gospel?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For I have received of the Lord, that which I also delivered unto you, (1 Corinthians 11:23)

**Q:** And should these be observed?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** And keep the ordinances as I delivered them unto you, (1 Corinthians 11:2)

**Q:** But is the moral law the principal rule of obedience?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** The Lord our God made a covenant with us in Horeb, (Deuteronomy 5:2)

### Section 3

**Q:** Was this law originally written upon the heart of man?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** They show the work of the law, written in their hearts, (Romans 2:15)

**Q:** Doth natural conscience enforce this law?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For the Gentiles, which have not the law, do by nature the things contained in the law, (Romans 2:14)

**Q:** Did the Gentiles then sin by breaking this law?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** What they know naturally in these things, they corrupt themselves, (Jude 10)

**Q:** And will they be punished for the breach of it?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** They that have sinned without law shall perish without law, (Romans 2:12)

**Q:** But is this law more fully revealed in the word?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** The Lord made not this covenant with our fathers, but with us, even us, who are all of us here alive this day, (Deuteronomy 5:3)

### Practical response

**Q:** Should we subscribe to the excellency of the law?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** The law is holy, just, and good, (Romans 7:12)

**Q:** And inquire into its uses?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Wherefore then serveth the law, (Galatians 3:19)

**Q:** Are the saints under the law as a covenant of works?  
**A:** No.  
**Proof:** We are not under the law, but under grace, (Romans 6:15)

**Q:** But are they under it as a rule of life?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Being not without law to God, (1 Corinthians 9:21)

**Q:** Is it universally binding?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** I esteem all thy precepts concerning all things to be right, (Psalm 119:128)

**Q:** And perpetually so?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Do we then make void the law through faith, God forbid, (Romans 3:31)

### Section 5

**Q:** Doth the law curb sin?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** It was made not for the righteous man but for the lawless and disobedient, (1 Timothy 1:9)

**Q:** And instruct in duty?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For the end of the commandment is charity, (1 Timothy 1:5)

**Q:** Doth it convince of sin?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** I had not known sin but by the law, (Romans 7:7)

**Q:** And aggravate sin?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** The law entered that the offence might abound, (Romans 5:20)

**Q:** And beget fear?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** It gendereth unto bondage, (Galatians 4:24)

**Q:** And silence carnal objections?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** That every mouth might be stopped, (Romans 3:19)

**Q:** And destroy a self-righteous principle?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** I through the law am dead to the law, (Galatians 2:19)

**Q:** And lead the sinner to Christ?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** The law is our school-master, to bring us to Christ, (Galatians 3:24)

**Q:** Is the law then of great advantage if rightly used?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For we know that the law is good, if a man use it lawfully, (1 Timothy 1:8)
