No; for seeing his Divinity is incomprehensible, and every where present, it followeth necessarily that the same is without the bounds of his humane Nature which he took unto him, and yet is nevertheless in it, and abideth personally united to it.

## Proofs
- No; for seeing his Divinity is incomprehensible, and (Acts 7:48,44; Acts 17:27; Jeremiah 23:23–24)
- every where present, it followeth necessarily that the same is without the bounds of his humane Nature which he took unto him, and yet is nevertheless in it, (Colossians 2:9; Matthew 28:6)
- and abideth personally united to it.
