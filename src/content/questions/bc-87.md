No mere man since the fall is able in this life perfectly to keep the commandments of God, but doth daily break them in thought, word, or deed.

## Proofs
- No mere man since the fall is able in this life perfectly to keep the commandments of God, (Ecclesiastes 7:20; 1 John 1:8, 10; Galatians 5:17)
- but doth daily break them in thought, word, or deed. (Genesis 6:5, 8:21; Rom 3:9–21; James 3:2–13)

## Additional Exposition

<a href="https://baptistcatechism.org/beddome/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">From Benjamin Beddome</a>


### Section 1

**Q:** Was Adam before the fall perfect?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For in the image of God created he him, (Genesis 1:27)

**Q:** Was Christ, who was not a mere man, perfect?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** He knew no sin, (2 Corinthians 5:21)

**Q:** And are the saints in heaven perfect?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** In their mouth is found no guile, (Revelation 14:5)

**Q:** But is any mere man since the fall in this life perfect?  
**A:** No.  
**Proof:** For there is not a just man on earth, (Ecclesiastes 7:20)

**Q:** And have the best of men acknowledged this?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** We are all as an unclean thing—and our iniquities like the wind have taken us away, (Isaiah 64:6)

### Section 2

**Q:** Did the Father of the faithful sin through unbelief?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For he denied his wife, lest the Egyptians should have slain him, (Genesis 12:12, 19)

**Q:** Was Jacob, a plain man, guilty of deceit?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For Isaac said, art thou my very son Esau? and he said I am, (Genesis 27:24)

**Q:** Did the meekest man offend by passion?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For they provoked his spirit so that he spoke unadvisedly with his lips, (Psalm 106:33)

**Q:** And the most patient man by impatience?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For he cursed his day, (Job 3:1)

**Q:** And was the wisest man guilty of the greatest folly?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For when Solomon was old his wives turned away his heart after other gods, (1 Kings 11:4)

**Q:** Did the most resolute of Christ’s disciples sin through weakness and fear?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For he began to curse and to swear, saying, I know not the man, (Mark 14:71)

**Q:** And did the apostle Paul, who so earnestly breathed after perfection, yet fall short of it?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** I am carnal, sold under sin, (Romans 7:14)

### Section 3

**Q:** Do the best of saints sin in thought?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Why think ye evil in your hearts? (Matthew 9:4)

**Q:** And in word?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Ye have not spoken of me the thing that is right, (Job 42:7)

**Q:** And in deed?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** By this deed thou hast given great occasion to the enemies of the Lord to blaspheme, (2 Samuel 12:14)

**Q:** Are they guilty of many sins which they know?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** My sin is ever before me, (Psalm 51:3)

**Q:** And of many more which they do not know?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For who can understand his errors? (Psalm 19:12)

### Section 4

**Q:** Are the saints sanctified in every part?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** In soul, body and spirit, (1 Thessalonians 5:23)

**Q:** And yet are they sanctified but in part?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For God will perfect that which is lacking, (1 Thessalonians 3:10)

**Q:** Are they sincerely obedient?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Remember I have walked before thee in truth, (Isaiah 38:3)

**Q:** But are they perfectly obedient?  
**A:** No.  
**Proof:** For we cannot do the things that we would, (Galatians 5:17)

**Q:** Have they a relative perfection in Christ?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** We are complete in him, (Colossians 2:10)

**Q:** But have they a personal perfection in themselves?  
**A:** No.  
**Proof:** Look not upon me because I am black, (Song of Solomon 1:6)

**Q:** Is justification then by our own works impossible?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For if thou, Lord, shouldst mark iniquity, who shall stand? (Psalm 103:4)
