The souls of believers are at their death made perfect in holiness, and do immediately pass into glory; and their bodies being still united to Christ, do rest in their graves till the resurrection.

## Proofs
- The souls of believers are at their death made perfect in holiness, (Hebrews 12:23)
- and do immediately pass into glory; (2 Corinthians 5:1, 6, 8; Philippians 1:23; Luke 23:43)
- and their bodies being still united to Christ, (1 Thessalonians 4:14)
- do rest in their graves (Isaiah 57:2)
- till the resurrection. (Job 19:26–27)

## Additional Exposition

### Section 1

**Q:** Do believers die as well as others?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Your fathers where are they, and the prophets do they live for ever? (Zechariah 1:5)

**Q:** Must they die as well as others?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Knowing that shortly I must put off this tabernacle, (2 Peter 1:14)

**Q:** Would they not live if they could?  
**A:** No.  
**Proof:** I would not live always, (Job 7:16)

**Q:** But is there a manifest difference between their death and that of others?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For precious in the sight of the Lord is the death of his saints, (Psalm 116:15)

### Section 2

**Q:** Is death an advantage to the saints?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** To die is gain, (Philippians 1:21)

**Q:** Doth the soul die with the body?  
**A:** No.  
**Proof:** For men may kill the body, but they are not able to kill the soul, (Matthew 10:28)

**Q:** Doth it sleep with the body?  
**A:** No.  
**Proof:** For it returns to God who gave it, (Ecclesiastes 12:7)

**Q:** Does it immediately enter upon a separate state?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For when we are absent from the body, we are present with the Lord, (2 Corinthians 5:8)

### Section 3

**Q:** Doth death release the saints from all their sorrows?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** They shall no more say, I am sick, (Isaiah 33:24)

**Q:** And free them from all their remaining corruptions?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For they are without fault before the throne of God, (Revelation 14:5)

**Q:** And set them at an eternal distance from all their spiritual enemies?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For the accuser of the brethren is cast down, (Revelation 12:10)

**Q:** And put an end to all their trials and labours?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** They rest from their labours, (Revelation 14:13)

**Q:** And introduce them into a state of absolute perfection?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For the spirits of just men are made perfect, (Hebrews 12:23)

### Section 4

**Q:** Do believers at death enter upon a state of perfect knowledge?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For here we see through a glass darkly, but then face to face, (1 Corinthians 13:12)

**Q:** And of perfect holiness?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For they have washed their robes, and made them white in the blood of the Lamb, (Revelation 7:14)

**Q:** And of complete glory?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Thou shalt guide me by thy counsel and afterward receive me to glory, (Psalm 73:24)

**Q:** Are they received into a glorious place?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For it is enlightened with the glory of God, and the Lamb is the light thereof, (Revelation 21:23)

**Q:** Are they joined with glorious company?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** They are come to an innumerable company of angels, to God the Judge of all, and to Jesus the Mediator of the new covenant, (Hebrews 12:22–23)

**Q:** Do they wear a glorious image?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** I shall be satisfied when I awake in thy likeness, (Psalm 17:15)

**Q:** Are they employed in glorious works?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** They serve God day and night, in his temple, (Revelation 7:15)

**Q:** And do they receive a glorious reward?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Ye shall receive a crown of glory, that fadeth not away, (1 Peter 5:4)

### Section 5

**Q:** Are the bodies of believers committed to the grave?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Then shall the dust return to the earth as it was, (Ecclesiastes 12:7)

**Q:** Is the grave the common receptacle of mankind?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Then shall the dust return to the earth as it was, (Ecclesiastes 12:7)

**Q:** Hath a decent interment been sometimes denied to wicked men?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** He shall be buried with the burial of an ass, (Jeremiah 22:10)

**Q:** But is it promised to the saint?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Thou shalt go to thy Father in peace, thou shalt be buried in a good old age, (Genesis 15:15)

**Q:** Is the grave a resting place to such?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** They shall rest in their beds, (Isaiah 57:2)

**Q:** And do they sometimes long for that resting place?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** O that thou wouldst hide me in the grave, (Job 14:13)

**Q:** And provide for it?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Give me a possession of a burying place, (Genesis 23:4)

### Section 6

**Q:** Doth death separate the saints from the love of God?  
**A:** No.  
**Proof:** Neither death nor life can separate us from the love of God, (Romans 8:38)

**Q:** Or dissolve their union with Christ?  
**A:** No.  
**Proof:** For they sleep in Jesus, (1 Thessalonians 4:14)

**Q:** May they therefore meet it without fear?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** O death, where is thy sting, 0 grave where is thy victory, (1 Corinthians 15:55)
