Christ as our Redeemer executeth the offices of a prophet, of a priest, and of king, both in his estate of humiliation and exaltation.

## Proofs
- Christ as our Redeemer executeth the offices of a prophet, of a priest, and of king, both in his estate of humiliation and exaltation. (Acts 3:22; Hebrews 12:25; 2 Corinthians 13:3; Hebrews 5:5–7; 7:25; Psalm 2:6; Isaiah 9:6–7; Matthew 21:5; Psalm 2:8–11)

## Additional Exposition

### Section 1

**Q:** Is the Saviour of the elect the anointed of God?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** God, thy God, hath anointed thee with the oil of gladness above thy fellows, (Psalm 45:7)

**Q:** Is he therefore called Christ?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** We have found the Messiah (that is being interpreted) the Christ, (John 1:41)

**Q:** Was Christ appointed for his work?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** He was fore-ordained before the foundation of the world, (1 Peter 1:20)

**Q:** Was he furnished with sufficient authority to enter upon it?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Him hath God the Father sealed, (John 6:27)

**Q:** Was he suitably qualified for the discharge of it?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** God gave not the Spirit by measure to him, (John 3:34)

**Q:** Was he owned and approved in it?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** This is my beloved Son, in whom I am well pleased, (Matthew 3:17)

**Q:** And did he put the finishing hand to it?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** I have finished the work that thou gavest me to do, (John 17:4)

### Section 2

**Q:** Was our salvation the great end of what Christ did and suffered?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** He suffered, the just for the unjust, that he might bring us to God, (1 Peter 3:18)

**Q:** Is he therefore to his people all they need?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Christ is all, (Colossians 3)

**Q:** Is he a priest?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** He is the High Priest of our profession, (Hebrews 3:1)

**Q:** Is he a king?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** I have set my king upon my holy hill of Zion, (Psalm 2:6)

### Section 3

**Q:** Doth Christ execute these offices wisely?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** My Servant shall deal prudently, (Isaiah 52:13)

**Q:** And faithfully?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** He was faithful to him that appointed him, (Hebrews 3:2)

**Q:** And diligently?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Wot ye not that I must be about my Father’s business? (Luke 2:49)

**Q:** And successfully?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** He shall not fail, nor be discouraged, (Isaiah 42:4)

### Section 4

**Q:** Did Christ execute these offices in his state of humiliation?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** I have glorified thee on the earth, (John 17:4)

**Q:** And doth he execute them in his-state of exaltation?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** He now appears in the presence of God for us, (Hebrews 9:24)

**Q:** Where Christ effectually executes one office, doth he execute all?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For he is both a prince and a Saviour, (Acts 5:31)

**Q:** And is he to be received in all?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Is Christ divided? (1 Corinthians 1:13)
