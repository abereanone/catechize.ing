Assurance of grace and salvation not being of the essence of faith, true believers may wait long before they obtain it; and, after the enjoyment thereof, may have it weakened and intermitted, through manifold distempers, sins, temptations, and desertions; yet are they never left without such a presence and support of the Spirit of God as keeps them from sinking into utter despair.

## Proofs
- Assurance of grace and salvation not being of the essence of faith, (Ephesians 1:13)
- true believers may wait long before they obtain it; (Isaiah 50:10; Psalm 88:1–3, 6–7, 9–10, 13–15)
- and, after the enjoyment thereof, may have it weakened and intermitted, through manifold distempers, sins, temptations, and desertions; (Psalm 77:1–12; Song of Solomon 5:2–3, 6; Psalms 51:8, 12; 31:22; 22:1)
- yet are they never left without such a presence and support of the Spirit of God as keeps them from sinking into utter despair. (1 John 3:9; Psalm 73:15, 23; Isaiah 54:7–10)
