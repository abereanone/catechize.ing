The third commandment forbiddeth all profaning and abusing of any thing whereby God makes himself known.

## Proofs
- The third commandment forbiddeth all profaning and abusing of any thing whereby God makes himself known. (Malachi 1:6–7, 12; 2:2; 3:14)

## Additional Exposition

### Section 1

**Q:** Is it a great sin to profane God’s name?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** They profaned my holy name, (Ezekiel 36:20)

**Q:** Is it a profanation of God’s name to use it as an expletive in discourse?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Let your communication be yea, yea, and nay, nay, for whatsoever is more than these cometh of evil, (Matthew 5:37)

**Q:** Or as a charm?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** As those exorcists who said we adjure you by Jesus, (Acts 19:13)

**Q:** Or as a sanction for cruelty?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Your brethren that hated you and cast you out, said, Let the Lord be glorified, (Isaiah 66:5)

**Q:** Is God’s name also profaned by putting a slight upon sacred things?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Cursed be he that voweth and sacrificeth to the Lord a corrupt thing, for I am a great King, saith the Lord, and my name is dreadful, (Malachi 1:14)

### Section 2

**Q:** Is blasphemy a breach of this commandment?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Foolish people have blasphemed thy name, (Psalm 74:18)

**Q:** And hypocrisy?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For unto the wicked God saith, what hast thou to do to declare my statutes, (Psalm 50:16)

**Q:** Is this a great sin?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Their heart is divided, now shall they be found faulty, (Hosea 10:2)

**Q:** And a destructive sin?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** The hypocrites in heart heap up wrath, (Job 36:13)

**Q:** Should we therefore be careful to avoid it?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Beware of the leaven of the Pharisees, which is hypocrisy, (Luke 12:1)

### Section 3

**Q:** Is this commandment broken by sinful and unnecessary lots?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** They cast pur, that is the lot, before Haman from day to day, (Esther 3:7)

**Q:** And by profane cursing?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Out of the same mouth proceedeth blessing and cursing, my brethren, these things ought not to be, (James 3:10)

**Q:** And by rash swearing?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Above all things, my brethren, swear not, (James 5:12)

**Q:** And by false swearing?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Ye shall not swear by my name falsely, (Leviticus 19:12)

### Section 4

**Q:** Is it a great sin to swear falsely?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For it blasphemeth that worthy name, by which we are called, (James 2:7)

**Q:** Is it a sin that brings a curse upon the persons who are guilty of it?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Will ye steal, murder and swear falsely, therefore I will cast you out of my sight, (Jeremiah 7:9, 15)

**Q:** And upon their families?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** It shall enter into the house of him that sweareth falsely by my name, (Zechariah 5:4)

**Q:** And upon the land?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Because of swearing the land mourns, (Jeremiah 23:10)
