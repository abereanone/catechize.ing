God is a Spirit, infinite, eternal, and unchangeable in his being, wisdom, power, holiness, justice, goodness, and truth.

## Proofs
- God is a Spirit, (John 4:24)
- infinite, (Job 11:7, 8, 9)
- eternal, (Psalm 90:2)
- and unchangeable (James 1:17)
- in his being, (Exodus 3:14)
- wisdom, (Psalm 147:5)
- power, (Revelation 4:8)
- holiness, (Revelation 15:4)
- justice, goodness, and truth. (Exodus 34:6)

## Additional Exposition

### Section 1

**Q:** Is God a Spirit?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** God is a Spirit, (John 4:24)

**Q:** Is he incorporeal and invisible?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Ye have not heard his voice at any time, nor seen his shape, (John 5:37)

**Q:** Is he a perfect spirit?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** He is light, and in him is no darkness, (1 John 1:5)

**Q:** Is he an active spirit?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** My Father worketh hitherto, (John 5:17)

**Q:** Is he a self-sufficient and independent spirit?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** His name is I am that I am (Exodus 3:14)

**Q:** Is he an immortal spirit?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Who only hath immortality, (1 Timothy 6:16)

**Q:** Is he above all other spirits?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** He is the Father of spirits, (Hebrews 12:9)

### Section 2

**Q:** Can we then form any adequate conception of the divine Being?  
**A:** No.  
**Proof:** What likeness can you compare unto him, (Isaiah 40:18)

**Q:** Are the organs of sense improperly ascribed unto him?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Hast thou eyes of flesh or seest thou as man seeth, (Job 10:4)

**Q:** Can he be gratified with carnal things?  
**A:** No.  
**Proof:** I will take no bullock out of thy house, nor he-goats out of thy fold, (Psalm 50:9)

**Q:** Should he be worshipped in a spiritual manner?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Whom I serve with my spirit, (Romans 1:9)

**Q:** May we expect spiritual blessings from him?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Who hath blessed us with all spiritual blessings, (Ephesians 1:3)

**Q:** And is he the only suitable portion for our spirits?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** The Lord is my portion saith my soul, (Lamentations 3:24)

### Section 3

**Q:** Is God infinite and omnipresent?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Whither shall I flee from thy presence, (Psalm 139:7)

**Q:** Is he in heaven?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** If I ascend up into heaven thou art there, (Psalm 139:8)

**Q:** But is he confined to heaven?  
**A:** No.  
**Proof:** The heaven of heavens cannot contain thee, (2 Chronicles 2:6)

**Q:** Is his essence diffused through the whole universe of things?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** He is above all and through all and in all, (Ephesians 4:6)

**Q:** Is he then near to every one of us?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** He is not far from every one of us, (Acts 17:27)

**Q:** Should this be a support to the saints under their burden?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Be strong for I am with you, saith the Lord, (Haggai 2:4)

**Q:** And a spur to holy actions?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Walk before me, and be thou perfect, (Genesis 17:1)

### Section 4

**Q:** Is God eternal?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** The eternal God is thy refuge, (Deuteronomy 33:27)

**Q:** Was he from everlasting?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Art thou not from everlasting, O Lord my God, (Habakkuk 1:12)

**Q:** Will he be to everlasting?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** From everlasting to everlasting thou art God, (Psalm 90:2)

**Q:** Is he only eternal?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** I am the first, and I am the last, and besides me there is no God, (Isaiah 44:6)

**Q:** Should he therefore be feared?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** He is the living God, and an everlasting King, at his wrath the earth shall tremble, (Jeremiah 10:10)

**Q:** And be trusted?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Trust ye in the Lord for ever, for in the Lord Jehovah is everlasting strength, (Isaiah 26:4)

**Q:** Is this perfection of God humbling to man?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Where wast thou when I laid the foundation of the earth, (Job 38:4)

**Q:** And glorious to himself?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Now to the King eternal, immortal, invisible, be honour and glory for ever and ever, (1 Timothy 1:17)

### Section 5

**Q:** Is God unchangeable?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Thou art the same, (Psalm 102:27)

**Q:** Is he absolutely so?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** With him there is no shadow of turning, (James 1:17)

**Q:** Is he so in his own nature and perfections?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** I am the Lord, I change not, (Malachi 3:6)

**Q:** And in his will?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** His Counsel shall stand, (Isaiah 46:10)

**Q:** And in his love?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** I have loved thee with an everlasting love, (Jeremiah 31:3)

**Q:** And in his special favours?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** The gifts and calling of God are without repentance, (Romans 11:29)

**Q:** May not God change through some unforeseen accident?  
**A:** No.  
**Proof:** For known unto God are all his works from the beginning of the world, (Acts 15:18)

**Q:** Or through want of power to effect what he has determined?  
**A:** No.  
**Proof:** For he is the Almighty God, (Genesis 17:1)

**Q:** Is it well for us that God is unchangeable?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Therefore the sons of Jacob are not consumed, (Malachi 3:6)

### Section 6

**Q:** Is God wise?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Counsel is mine and sound wisdom, (Proverbs 8:14)

**Q:** Doth his wisdom appear in creation and providence?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** He is wonderful in counsel, and excellent in working, (Isaiah 28:29)

**Q:** And in redemption?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Herein he hath abounded towards us in all wisdom and prudence, (Ephesians 1:8)

**Q:** Is he infinitely wise?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** His understanding is infinite, (Psalm 147:5)

**Q:** Is he originally so?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** With whom took he counsel, and who instructed him, (Isaiah 40:14)

**Q:** Is he perfectly so?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** The foolishness of God is wiser than man, (1 Corinthians 1:25)

**Q:** And is he incomprehensibly so?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** O the depth of the riches, both of the wisdom and knowledge of God, (Romans 11:33)

**Q:** Is there any counterworking his wisdom?  
**A:** No.  
**Proof:** There is no wisdom, nor understanding, nor counsel, against the Lord, (Proverbs 21:30)

**Q:** Doth all wisdom proceed from him?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** He giveth wisdom unto the wise, (Daniel 2:21)

**Q:** Should we therefore seek it at his hands?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** If any man lack wisdom, let him ask it of God, (James 1:5)

### Section 7

**Q:** Is God powerful?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Power belongeth unto God, (Psalm 62:11)

**Q:** Can he do all things?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Is there any thing too hard for me, (Jeremiah 32:27)

**Q:** Can he do all things with the greatest ease?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** He taketh up the isles as a very little thing, (Isaiah 40:15)

**Q:** Can he do all things in the most perfect manner?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** He is a rock, his work is perfect, (Deuteronomy 32:4)

**Q:** Is God’s power necessary to give a sanction to his laws?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** There is one Lawgiver, who is able to save and to destroy, (James 4:12)

**Q:** Is it irresistible?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** None can stay his hand, (Daniel 4:35)

**Q:** Is it subject to decay?  
**A:** No.  
**Proof:** The Lord’s hand is not shortened, that it cannot save, (Isaiah 59:1)

**Q:** Does it extend to evil spirits and wicked men?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** The deceiver and deceived are his, (Job 12:16)

**Q:** Is it then a great folly to sin against such a God?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Do we provoke the Lord to jealousy, are we stronger than he, (1 Corinthians 10:22)

### Section 8

**Q:** Is God holy?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** He is the holy one of Israel, (Isaiah 41:21)

**Q:** Is he supremely holy?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** There is none holy as the Lord, (1 Samuel 2:2)

**Q:** Does the holiness of God particularly appear in the work of redemption?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Zion shall be redeemed with judgment, and her converts with righteousness, (Isaiah 1:27)

**Q:** Have the heathens by the light of nature acknowledged the holiness of God?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** I know that the spirit of the holy Gods is in thee, (Daniel 4:9)

**Q:** And do spirits above make it the matter of their praise?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** They continually say, Holy, holy, holy, Lord God Almighty, (Revelation 4:8)

**Q:** And should we imitate them herein?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Bless his holy name, (Psalm 103:1)

### Section 9

**Q:** Is God just?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** The Lord is righteous, (Psalm 129:4)

**Q:** Is he absolutely and perfectly so?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Is there unrighteousness with God? God forbid, (Romans 9:14)

**Q:** Is he just in inflicting temporal judgments?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** O Lord, righteousness belongeth unto thee, but unto us confusion of face, (Daniel 9:7)

**Q:** And eternal vengeance?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Is God unrighteous, who taketh vengeance, (Romans 3:5)

**Q:** Does the justice of God eminently appear in the undertaking and sufferings of Christ?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Whom God hath set forth to be a propitiation through faith in his blood to declare his righteousness, (Romans 3:25)

**Q:** Should we then acknowledge the justice of God?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Howbeit thou art just, (Nehemiah 9:33)

**Q:** And seek to avoid the direful effects of it?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Escape for thy life, lest thou be consumed, (Genesis 19:17)

### Section 10

**Q:** Is God good?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Thou, Lord, art good, (Psalm 86:5)

**Q:** May his goodness excite our wonder?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** How great is his goodness, (Zechariah 9:17)

**Q:** And should it excite our gratitude?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** O give thanks unto the Lord, for he is good, (Psalm 106:1)

**Q:** Is God universally good?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** He is good to all, (Psalm 145:9)

**Q:** Is he good to sinners?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** He sendeth rain on the just and on the unjust, (Matthew 5:45)

**Q:** Is he in an especial manner good to his own people?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** That I may see the good of thy chosen, (Psalm 106:5)

**Q:** Doth God take the contempt of his goodness heinously?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Or despisest thou the riches of his goodness, (Romans 2:4)

### Section 11

**Q:** Is God a God of truth?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** These things saith he that is true, (Revelation 3:7)

**Q:** Is he true to his promises?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Thou wilt perform the truth to Jacob, (Micah 7:20)

**Q:** And to his threatenings?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** But my words, did not they take hold of your fathers? (Zechariah 1:6)

**Q:** And to Christ?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Once have I sworn by my holiness that I will not lie unto David, (Psalm 89:35)

**Q:** Is God unchangeable in his truth?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** The truth of the Lord endureth for ever, (Exodus 7:2)

**Q:** Should we therefore believe God?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** We should judge him faithful who hath promised, (Hebrews 11:11)

**Q:** And that rather than man?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Let God be true, but every man a liar, (Romans 3:4)

**Q:** But though we believe not, does God continue faithful?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For what if some did not believe, shall their unbelief make the faith of God without effect? (Romans 3:3)
