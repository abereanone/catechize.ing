Baptism is to be administered to all those who actually profess repentance towards God, faith in and obedience to our Lord Jesus Christ, and to none other.

## Proofs
- Baptism is to be administered to all those who actually profess repentance towards God, (Acts 2:38; Matthew 3:6)
- faith in and obedience to our Lord Jesus Christ, and to none other. (Acts 8:12, 36–38; 10:47–48)

## Additional Exposition

<a href="https://baptistcatechism.org/beddome/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">From Benjamin Beddome</a>


### Section 1

**Q:** Is baptism to be administered to such who repent?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** It is the baptism of repentance, (Mark 1:4)

**Q:** And to all such?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Repent and be baptized every one of you, (Acts 2:37)

**Q:** And to none but such?  
**A:** No.  
**Proof:** O generation of vipers, who hath warned you to flee from the wrath to come? (Matthew 3:7)

**Q:** Should this repentance be actually professed?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** They were baptized in Jordan, confessing their sins, (Matthew 3:6)

**Q:** And appear in its fruits?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Bring forth fruits meet for repentance, (Matthew 3:8)

**Q:** And have the worst of sinners upon their repentance a right to baptism?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Then came also publicans to be baptized, (Luke 3:12)

### Practical response

**Q:** Should faith be before baptism?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** The Corinthians believed and were baptized, (Acts 18:8)

**Q:** Will a credible profession of faith justify the administration of this ordinance?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For Simon Magus believed and was baptized, (Acts 8:13)

**Q:** But will real faith only justify a submission to it?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** If thou believest with all thine heart thou mayest, (Acts 8:37)

**Q:** And will the ordinance be unavailable where faith is wanting?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For whatsoever is not of faith is sin, (Romans 14:23)

### Section 3

**Q:** Must persons first be taught before they are baptized?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Go teach all nations, baptizing them, (Matthew 28:19)

**Q:** And appear to be partakers of the Holy Ghost?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Can any man forbid water that these should not be baptized, who have received the Holy Ghost as well as we? (Acts 10:47)

**Q:** And gladly receive the word?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** They that gladly received the word were baptized, (Acts 2:41)

**Q:** And discover a disposition to obey?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Men and brethren, what shall we do? And Peter said, repent and be baptized, (Acts 2:37)

### Practical response

**Q:** Should baptism be a matter of choice in those that submit to it?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** See there is water, what doth hinder me to be baptized? (Acts 8:36)

**Q:** And their own act and deed?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** They went out to him and were baptized, (Mark 1:5)

**Q:** And the answer of a good conscience?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For baptism saveth us, as it is the answer of a good conscience towards God, (1 Peter 3:21)

**Q:** And where these qualifications are wanting, should baptism be refused?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Give not that which is holy unto dogs, (Matthew 7:6)
