In the fourth petition, which is, Give us this day our daily bread, we pray that of God’s free gift we may receive a competent portion of the good things of this life, and enjoy his blessing with them.

## Proofs
- In the fourth petition, which is, Give us this day our daily bread, (Matthew 6:11)
- we pray that of God’s free gift we may receive a competent portion of the good things of this life, and enjoy his blessing with them. (Proverbs 30:8; Genesis 28:20; 1 Timothy 4:4–5)

## Additional Exposition

### Section 1

**Q:** Are temporal blessings forfeited?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Cursed is the ground for thy sake, (Genesis 3:17)

**Q:** But are they promised?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** My God shall supply all your wants, (Philippians 4:19)

**Q:** Should we therefore pray for them?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** O that thou wouldst bless me indeed, and enlarge my coast, (1 Chronicles 4:10)

### Practical response

**Q:** Should we pray for temporal blessings with religious views?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** That we may give to him that needeth, (Ephesians 4:28)

**Q:** And should we only pray for a competency of them?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Feed me with food convenient for me, (Proverbs 30:8)

**Q:** But above all should we entreat God’s blessing upon them?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** And it came to pass as he sat at meat with them, he took bread and blessed it, and gave to them, (Luke 24:30)

**Q:** And are they then like to be most comfortable to us?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For every creature is sanctified by the word of God and prayer, (1 Timothy 4:5)

### Practical response

**Q:** Should we pray for the comforts of life believingly?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Thou saidst, I will surely do thee good, (Genesis 32:12)

**Q:** And yet submissively?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** If he say, I have no delight in thee; behold here I am, let him do to me as seemeth good unto him, (2 Samuel 15:26)

**Q:** Should our prayers be attended with suitable endeavours?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For thou hast eat the labour of thine hands, (Psalm 128:2)

**Q:** And should we pray for others as well as ourselves?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Forget not the congregation of the poor, (Psalm 74:19)

### Practical response

**Q:** Should the bread that we pray for be our own bread?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** That we may eat our own bread, (2 Thessalonians 3:12)

**Q:** And not the bread of deceit?  
**A:** No.  
**Proof:** For, though the bread of deceit is sweet to a man, yet afterwards his mouth shall be filled with gravel, (Proverbs 20:17)

**Q:** Nor the bread of oppression?  
**A:** No.  
**Proof:** For God shall break in pieces the oppressor, (Psalm 72:4)

**Q:** Nor the bread of idleness?  
**A:** No.  
**Proof:** For ’tis said of the virtuous woman, that she eateth not the bread of idleness, (Proverbs 31:27)

**Q:** But above all, must we pray for the bread of our souls?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Lord, evermore give us this bread, (John 6:34)

### Section 5

**Q:** Doth this petition exclude an anxious thoughtfulness for futurity?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Take no thought for the morrow, (Matthew 6:34)

**Q:** But doth it forbid a prudent thoughtfulness?  
**A:** No.  
**Proof:** Go to the ant, thou sluggard, consider her ways—she provideth her meat in the summer, (Proverbs 6:6, 8)

**Q:** Doth it teach us to cast all our care upon God?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For he careth for us, (1 Peter 5:7)

**Q:** And to look to him for success in our several callings?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Establish thou the work of our hands upon us, (Psalm 90:17)

**Q:** And to ascribe all our prosperity to him?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Thou shalt remember the Lord thy God, for it is he that giveth thee power to get wealth, (Deuteronomy 8)
