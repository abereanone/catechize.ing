The reasons annexed to the second commandment are, God’s sovereignty over us, his propriety in us, and the zeal he hath to his own worship.

## Proofs
- The reasons annexed to the second commandment are, God’s sovereignty over us, (Psalm 45:2–3, 6)
- his propriety in us, (Psalm 45:11)
- and the zeal he hath to his own worship. (Exodus 34:13–14)

## Additional Exposition

### Section 1

**Q:** Is God the Lord?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** I am the Lord, (Leviticus 19:4)

**Q:** Is that a reason why we should worship him?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Let us kneel before the Lord our Maker, (Psalm 95:5)

**Q:** And is idolatry therefore an act of rebellion against him?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** We have rebelled by departing from thee, (Daniel 9:5)

**Q:** Is he our God?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** I am the Lord your God, (Leviticus 26:13)

**Q:** Is that also a reason why we should worship him?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** We will serve the Lord, for he is our God, (Joshua 24:18)

**Q:** And cleave unto him?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For hath a nation changed their gods, (Jeremiah 2:11)

**Q:** And is it a breach of covenant if we do not?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** They will turn unto other gods, and serve them, and break my covenant, (Deuteronomy 31:20)

### Section 2

**Q:** Is God a jealous God?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** The Lord thy God is a jealous God amongst you, (Deuteronomy 6:15)

**Q:** Doth jealousy imply the height of anger and indignation?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** God is jealous, the Lord revengeth, and is furious, (Nahum 1:2)

**Q:** And doth nothing stir up the jealousy of God more than idolatry?  
**A:** No.  
**Proof:** They have moved me to jealousy with that which is not God, they have provoked me to anger with their vanities, (Deuteronomy 32:21)

### Section 3

**Q:** Will God visit the iniquity of such?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Shall I not visit them for these things, (Jeremiah 5:9)

**Q:** And that upon their children?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Thou recompensest the iniquity of the fathers, into the bosom of their children, (Jeremiah 32:18)

**Q:** And their children’s children?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** To the third and fourth generation, (Exodus 34:7)

**Q:** But will his judgments fall most heavily on those who make their father’s sins their own?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Behold ye are risen up in your father’s stead, an increase of sinful men, to augment the anger of the Lord, (Numbers 32:14)

### Section 4

**Q:** Amidst the most general apostacy are there some that love God* and keep his commandments?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** I have left me seven thousand in Israel that have not bowed to Baal, (1 Kings 19:18)

**Q:** And may such expect to be exempted from desolating judgments when they come?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Go set a mark on the forehead of them that sigh and cry, (Ezekiel 9:4)

**Q:** And to meet with peculiar tokens of the divine regard?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For the mercy of the Lord is from everlasting to everlasting, upon all them that fear him, (Psalm 103:17)
