The sum of the ten commandments is, to love the Lord our God, with all our heart, with all our soul, with all our strength, and with all our mind; and our neighbour as ourselves.

## Proofs
- The sum of the ten commandments is, to love the Lord our God, with all our heart, with all our soul, with all our strength, and with all our mind; and our neighbour as ourselves. (Matthew 22:37–40)

## Additional Exposition

<a href="https://baptistcatechism.org/beddome/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">From Benjamin Beddome</a>


### Section 1

**Q:** Were the Ten Commandments written originally in two tables?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** The Lord said hew thee out two tables of stone, and I will write upon the tables, (Deuteronomy 10:1)

**Q:** Is love to God inclusive of all the duties of the first table?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** It is the first and great commandment, (Matthew 22:38)

**Q:** And is love to our neighbour inclusive of all the duties of the second?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** The second is like unto it, thou shalt love thy neighbour as thyself, (Matthew 22:39)

**Q:** Is the whole law then fulfilled in love?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For love is the fulfilling of the law, (Romans 13:10)

### Section 2

**Q:** Is it our duty to love God?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Take good heed to yourselves that ye love the Lord your God, (Joshua 23:11)

**Q:** Is there in him every thing that is lovely?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** How great is his beauty, (Zechariah 9:17)

**Q:** And will he return our love?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** I love them that love me, (Proverbs 8:17)

**Q:** And reward it?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For eye hath not seen what God hath prepared for them that love him, (1 Corinthians 2:9)

### Practical response

**Q:** Should we discover our love to God in frequent thoughts of him?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** My meditation of him shall be sweet, (Psalm 104:34)

**Q:** And in earnest desires after him?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** My soul thirsteth for thee, my flesh longeth for thee, (Psalm 63:2)

**Q:** And in sorrow for his absence?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Why hidest thou thyself, (Psalm 10:1)

**Q:** And in an unfeigned submission to his providential will?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** I was dumb, I opened not my mouth, (Psalm 39:9)

**Q:** And in a conscientious obedience to all his commands?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** This is the love of God, that we keep his commandments, (1 John 5:3)

**Q:** And in a due resentment against whatsoever offends him?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Do not I hate them that hate thee, and am I not grieved with those that rise up against thee? (Psalm 139:21)

### Practical response

**Q:** Should our love to God be rational and judicious?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** That your love might abound yet more and more, in knowledge and in all judgment, (Philippians 1:9)

**Q:** And upright and sincere?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Grace be with them that love the Lord Jesus Christ in sincerity, (Ephesians 6:24)

**Q:** And strong and vehement?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** I am sick of love, (Song of Solomon 2:5)

**Q:** And supreme and superlative?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** He that loveth father or mother more than me, is not worthy of me, (Matthew 10:37)

**Q:** And constant and abiding?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Keep yourselves in the love of God, (Jude 21)

### Section 5

**Q:** Must our love be confined to God?  
**A:** No.  
**Proof:** He that loveth God must love his brother also, (1 John 4:21)

**Q:** Should we seek the good of others?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Charity seeketh not her own, (1 Corinthians 13:5)

**Q:** And lend a compassionate ear to their complaints?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** To the afflicted pity should be shown, (Job 6:14)

**Q:** And supply their necessities?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** We should not withhold the poor from his desire, nor cause the eye of the widow to fail, (Job 31:16)

**Q:** And reprove their sins?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Thou shalt in any wise rebuke thy neighbour, (Leviticus 19:17)

**Q:** And pray for them?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Pray one for another, (James 5:16)

**Q:** And even venture our lives for them if it be necessary?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** We ought to lay down our lives for the brethren, (1 John 3:16)

### Practical response

**Q:** Should we love others as Christ hath loved us?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** And walk in love as Christ also loved us, (Ephesians 5:2)

**Q:** And as we love ourselves?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Thou shalt love thy neighbour as thyself, (Matthew 19:19)

**Q:** Should we love them as sincerely?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Let us not love in word, but in deed and in truth, (1 John 3:18)

**Q:** And as fervently?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Above all things have fervent charity among yourselves, (1 Peter 4:8)

**Q:** And as constantly?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Let brotherly love continue, (Hebrews 13:1)

**Q:** Doth it hence follow that we . should do to others as we would be done by?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Whatsoever ye would that men should do to you, do ye even unto them, (Matthew 7:12)

### Section 7

**Q:** Is love, Christ’s new commandment?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** A new commandment I give unto you, that ye love one another, (John 13:34)

**Q:** Is it a test of our discipleship?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** By this shall all men know that ye are my disciples, if ye love one another, (John 13:35)

**Q:** Is it placed at the top of the Christian graces?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** The greatest of these is charity, (1 Corinthians 13:13)

**Q:** And is it a grace that will continue and accompany us to heaven?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Now abideth charity, (1 Corinthians 13:13)
