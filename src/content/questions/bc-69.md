The fifth commandment requireth the preserving the honour and performing the duties belonging to every one in their several places and relations, as superiors, inferiors, or equals.

## Proofs
- The fifth commandment requireth the preserving the honour and performing the duties belonging to every one in their several places and relations, as superiors, (Ephesians 5:21)
- inferiors, (1 Peter 2:17)
- or equals. (Romans 12:10)

### Practical response

**Q:** Should parents provide for their children?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** What man is there among you, who if his son ask bread, will give him a stone? (Matthew 7:9)

**Q:** And instruct them?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Train up a child in the way he should go, (Proverbs 22:6)

**Q:** And correct them when needful?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** The rod and reproof give wisdom, (Proverbs 29:15)

**Q:** But should they abstain, from severity and passion?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Parents, provoke not your children to anger, (Colossians 3:21)

**Q:** Is it the duty of parents to pray for their children?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Give unto Solomon, my son, a perfect heart, (1 Chronicles 29:19)

**Q:** And to pray with them?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Thus David returned to bless his household, (2 Samuel 6:20)

**Q:** And to set them a good example?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** I will walk within my house with a perfect heart, (Psalm 101:2)

**Q:** And to keep a watchful eye over them?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** It may be my sons have sinned and cursed God in their hearts, (Job 1:5)

**Q:** And will this in the end turn to their own advantage?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For he that begetteth a wise child shall have joy of him, (Proverbs 23:24)

### Practical response

**Q:** Should servants honour their masters?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** They must count them worthy of all honour, (1 Timothy 6:1)

**Q:** And obey them?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Ye servants obey in all things your masters, (Colossians 3:22)

**Q:** And be just and faithful to them?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Showing all good fidelity, (Titus 2:10)

**Q:** And patiently bear their reproofs and corrections?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Not answering again, (Titus 2:9)

**Q:** Is this subjection due even to froward masters?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** To the froward also, (1 Peter 2:18)

**Q:** And is it equally so to believing ones?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** They that have believing masters let them not despise them, but rather do them service, (1 Timothy 6:2)

### Practical response

**Q:** Should masters be just to their servants?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Masters, give to your servants, that which is just and good, (Colossians 4:1)

**Q:** And gentle?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Forbearing threatening, (Ephesians 6:9)

**Q:** But should they be careful to maintain their authority?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For the earth is disquieted for a servant when he reigneth, (Proverbs 30:22)

### Section 4

**Q:** Is it the duty of husbands to love their wives?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Husbands, love your wives, (Ephesians 5:25)

**Q:** And of wives to submit to their husbands?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Wives, submit yourselves unto your own husbands, (Ephesians 5:22)

**Q:** Is it the duty of husbands and wives to be pleasing one to another?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** He that is married careth how to please his wife, and she that is married how to please her husband, (1 Corinthians 7:33–34)

**Q:** And to be mutually helpful to one another in their religious concerns?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** How knowest thou, 0 wife, whether them shalt save thy husband, or how knowest thou, 0 man, whether thou shalt save thy wife, (1 Corinthians 7:16)

### Section 5

**Q:** Is there a duty due from subjects to their magistrates?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Honour the king, (1 Peter 2:17)

**Q:** And from magistrates to their subjects?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** He that ruleth over men must be just, (2 Samuel 23:3)

**Q:** Is there a duty due from people to their ministers?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Obey them that have the rule over you, and submit yourselves, (Hebrews 13:17)

**Q:** And from ministers to their people?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Feed the flock of God, which is among you, (1 Peter 5:2)

### Section 6

**Q:** Is it the duty of inferiors to honour their superiors?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Thou shalt rise up before the hoary head, and honour the face of the old man, (Leviticus 19:32)

**Q:** And to endeavour to improve by them?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** They waited for me as for the rain, (Job 29:23)

**Q:** Is it the duty of superiors to carry it respectfully towards their inferiors?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Condescend to men of low estate, (Romans 12:16)

**Q:** And to seek their edification?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** The fruit of the righteous is a tree of life, (Proverbs 11:30)

### Practical response

**Q:** Should neighbours show themselves neighbourly?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Let every one please his neighbour for his good, (Romans 15:2)

**Q:** And friends behave themselves friendly?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** A man that hath friends must show himself friendly, (Proverbs 18:24)

**Q:** And brethren carry themselves in a brotherly manner?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Behold how good and pleasant a thing it is for brethren to dwell together in unity, (Psalm 133:1)

**Q:** And does a failure on one side justify a failure on the other?  
**A:** No.  
**Proof:** See that none render evil for evil to any man, (1 Thessalonians 5:15)
