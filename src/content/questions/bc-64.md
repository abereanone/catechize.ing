Before the resurrection of Christ, God appointed the seventh day of the week to be the weekly Sabbath; and the first day of the week ever since, to continue to the end of the world, which is the Christian Sabbath.

## Proofs
- Before the resurrection of Christ, God appointed the seventh day of the week to be the weekly Sabbath; (Exodus 20:8–11; Deuteronomy 5:12–14)
- and the first day of the week ever since, to continue to the end of the world, which is the Christian Sabbath. (Psalm 118:24; Matthew 28:1; Mark 2:27–28; John 20:19–20, 26; Revelation 1:10; Mark 16:2; Luke 24:1, 30–36; John 20:1; Acts 1:3; 2:1–2; 20:7; 1 Corinthians 16:1–2)

## Additional Exposition

### Section 1

**Q:** Was the Sabbath appointed from the beginning of the world?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** God blessed the seventh day and sanctified it, (Genesis 2:2)

**Q:** And was it observed before the giving of the law?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Tomorrow is the rest of the holy Sabbath, (Exodus 16:23)

**Q:** Was it particularly enforced upon the Israelites?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** I gave them my Sabbaths, (Ezekiel 20:12)

**Q:** And that in commemoration of their delivery out of Egypt?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Remember that thou wast a servant in Egypt, and that the Lord thy God brought thee out from thence, therefore he commanded thee to keep the Sabbath-day, (Deuteronomy 5:15)

**Q:** But is it obligator yon Christians?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Pray that your flight be not on the Sabbath-day, (Matthew 24:20)

### Section 2

**Q:** Was the Jewish Sabbath kept on the seventh day of the week?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For he spake on the seventh day this wise, (Hebrews 4:4)

**Q:** But had Christ a power to alter it?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For the Son of Man is Lord even of the Sabbath-day, (Matthew 12:8)

**Q:** Is such an alteration hinted at in the Old Testament?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** It shall be that upon the eighth day, and so forward, the priests shall make your burnt-offerings, etc., and I will accept you, saith the Lord God. (Ezekiel 43:27)

**Q:** And did it actually take place after Christ’s resurrection?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For on the first day of the week, the disciples came together to break bread, (Acts 20:7)

**Q:** And does their example carry in it, the force of a command?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Wherefore I beseech you, be ye followers of me, (1 Corinthians 4:16)

### Section 3

**Q:** Did Christ rise upon the first day?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** He rose early on the first day of the week, (Mark 16:9)

**Q:** And did he then rest from his labours?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** He ceased from his own works, as God did from his, (Hebrews 4:10)

**Q:** Did he honour this day with his presence?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** The same day at evening, being the first day of the week, came Jesus, and stood in the midst, (John 20:19)

**Q:** And did he renew his visit the next first day?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** After eight days, when the disciples were within and Thomas with them, came Jesus, (John 20:26)

**Q:** And was this a mark of his approbation?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For he meeteth them that remember him in his ways, (Isaiah 64:5)

### Section 4

**Q:** Was this day dignified by a new title?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** It was called the Lord’s day, (Revelation 1:10)

**Q:** Did the Spirit bestow his extraordinary gifts on this day?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For it was on the day of Pentecost, which was the morrow after the seventh day, (Acts 2:1)

**Q:** Does he on it confer his special grace?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** I was in the Spirit on the Lord’s day, (Revelation 1:10)

### Section 5

**Q:** Did the primitive Christians assemble on this day?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Upon the first day of the week let every one lay by him in store, (1 Corinthians 16:1–2)

**Q:** And was this a public and not a private collection?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** That there be no gatherings when I come. (1 Corinthians 16:1–2)

**Q:** Is their practice then a rule?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Ye became followers of the churches of Christ, (1 Thessalonians 2:14)

### Section 6

**Q:** Do we upon this day commemorate a more glorious creation than the first?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Behold I create new heavens and a new earth, and the former shall not be remembered, nor come into mind, (Isaiah 65:17)

**Q:** And celebrate a more eminent deliverance than that of the children of Israel out of Egypt?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** The days come that they shall no more say, the Lord liveth which brought up the children of Israel out of Egypt, (Jeremiah 23:7)

**Q:** Should we therefore join with our fellow Christians in keeping this day?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For if any man will be contentious, we have no such custom, neither the churches of Christ, (1 Corinthians 11:16)

**Q:** But is not this a direct breach of the fourth commandment?  
**A:** No.  
**Proof:** For that only says, Six days shalt thou labour, but the seventh (i.e. the day following the sixth) is the sabbath, (Exodus 20:10)

**Q:** If the matter was more disputable than it is, should not the superior advantage of keeping the first day in the present circumstances of things have some weight?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For the sabbath was made for man, and not man for the sabbath, (Mark 2:27)
