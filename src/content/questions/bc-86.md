The tenth commandment forbiddeth all discontentment with our own estate, envying or grieving at the good of our neighbour, and all inordinate motions and affections to anything that is his.

## Proofs
- The tenth commandment forbiddeth all discontentment with our own estate, (1 Kings 21:4; Esther 5:13; 1 Corinthians 10:10)
- envying or grieving at the good of our neighbour, (Galatians 5:26; James 3:14,16)
- and all inordinate motions and affections to anything that is his. (Romans 7:7–8, 13:9; Deuteronomy 5:21)

## Additional Exposition

### Section 1

**Q:** Is discontent a sin?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** The foolishness of man perverteth his way, and his heart fretteth against the Lord, (Proverbs 19:3)

**Q:** Can it make our condition better?  
**A:** No.  
**Proof:** For which of you can by taking thought can add one cubit to his stature, (Matthew 6:27)

**Q:** And will it certainly make it worse?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** All this availeth me nothing so long as I see Mordecai the Jew sitting in the king’s gate, (Esther 5:13)

**Q:** Is it a sin to undervalue present mercies?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** As those did who despised the pleasant land, (Psalm 106:24)

**Q:** Or to aggravate present afflictions?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** As Hezekiah, who said, I reckoned till morning, that as a lion, so will he break all my bones, (Isaiah 38:13)

**Q:** Is a discontented spirit from Satan?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Yea hath God said ye shall not eat of every tree of the garden, (Genesis 3:1)

**Q:** Is a brand of ignominy affixed to it?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** These are murmurers, complainers, (Jude 16)

**Q:** And is a curse entailed upon it?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Let them wander up and down for meat, and grudge if they be not satisfied, (Psalm 59:15)

### Section 2

**Q:** Is envy a sin?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Let us walk honestly as in the day, not in strife and envying, (Romans 13:13)

**Q:** Is it a sin for the wicked to envy the just?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** They shall see and be ashamed, for their envy at thy people, (Isaiah 26:11)

**Q:** Or for the just to envy the wicked?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Envy not the oppresser, (Proverbs 3:31)

**Q:** And is it a sin to envy for the sake of others?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Envyest thou for my sake? (Numbers 11:29)

### Section 3

**Q:** Is covetousness a great sin?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Covetousness, let it not be once named among you, as becometh saints, (Ephesians 5:3)

**Q:** And a great vexation?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For he that loveth silver shall not be satisfied with silver, (Ecclesiastes 5:10)

**Q:** Is it a hurt to our families?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** He that is greedy of gain troubleth his own house, (Proverbs 15:27)

**Q:** And an injury to our neighbours?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Thus Ahab’s covetousness caused Naboth’s murder, (1 Kings 21:1)

**Q:** And above all an affront to God?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For it is he that maketh poor and maketh rich, (1 Samuel 2:7)

**Q:** Is it a sin that rarely goes unpunished in this world?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For the iniquity of his covetousness I was wroth and smote him, (Isaiah 57:17)

**Q:** But will it be severely punished in the world to come?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For the covetous shall not inherit the kingdom of God, (1 Corinthians 6:9)

**Q:** Should we therefore strive against it?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Thou, O man of God, flee these things, (1 Timothy 6:11)

**Q:** And pray against it?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Incline my heart unto thy testimonies and not to covetousness, (Psalm 119:36)

### Section 4

**Q:** May we desire unlawful enjoyments?  
**A:** No.  
**Proof:** We must not lust after evil things, (1 Corinthians 10:6)

**Q:** Or lawful enjoyments inordinately?  
**A:** No.  
**Proof:** Labour not for the meat that perisheth, (John 6:27)

**Q:** Doth all sin begin in the desires of the heart?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For sinners walk in the way of their heart, (Ecclesiastes 11:9)

**Q:** Should we therefore keep a constant watch over it?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Keep thine heart with all diligence, for out of it are the issues of life, (Proverbs 4:23)
