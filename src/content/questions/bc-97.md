Baptism is an ordinance of the New Testament instituted by Jesus Christ, to be unto the party baptized a sign of his fellowship with him, in his death, burial, and resurrection; of his being ingrafted into him; of remission of sins; and of his giving up himself unto God through Jesus Christ, to live and walk in newness of life.

## Proofs
- Baptism is an ordinance of the New Testament instituted by Jesus Christ, to be unto the party baptized a sign of his fellowship with him, in his death, burial, and resurrection; of his being ingrafted into him; (Romans 6:3–5; Colossians 2:12; Galatians 3:27)
- of remission of sins; (Mark 1:4; Acts 2:38, 22:16)
- and of his giving up himself unto God through Jesus Christ, to live and walk in newness of life. (Romans 6:3–4)

## Additional Exposition

### Section 1

**Q:** Doth the scripture speak of a baptism with the Ghost?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** He shall baptize you with the Holy Ghost, (Matthew 3:11)

**Q:** And was this accomplished?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** They were filled with the Holy Ghost, (Acts 2:4)

**Q:** Doth it also speak of a baptism of sufferings?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Are ye able to be baptized with the baptism that I am baptized with? (Matthew 20:22)

**Q:** And was Christ indeed eminently baptized therewith?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** I am come into the deep waters where the floods overflow me, (Psalm 69:2)

**Q:** But is there besides these a baptism with water?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** I baptize you with water, (Matthew 3:11)

### Section 2

**Q:** Did the Jews baptize before John?  
**A:** No.  
**Proof:** For they ask why baptizest thou, if thou be not the Christ, nor Elias? (John 1:25)

**Q:** Did John’s baptism introduce the gospel dispensation?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** The beginning of the gospel of Jesus Christ —John did baptize, (Mark 1:1, 4)

**Q:** And had it a reference to a Saviour shortly to come?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** He said, that they should believe on him, which should come after him, that is on Christ Jesus, (Acts 19:4)

**Q:** Was John sent of God, to baptize?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** He that sent me to baptize with water, the same said unto me, (John 1:33)

**Q:** Did the people receive his baptism as from God?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** They justified God, being baptized with the baptism of (Luke 7:29)

**Q:** And was John’s baptism the same for substance as that of Christ?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For Jesus baptized, and John also was baptizing, (John 3:22–23)

### Section 3

**Q:** Was Christ himself baptized?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Then cometh Jesus unto John to be baptized of him, (Matthew 3:13)

**Q:** Did he thereby confer an honour upon this ordinance?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For there came a voice from heaven, saying, This is my beloved Son, in whom I am well pleased, (Matthew 3:17)

**Q:** And is this a powerful engagement upon us to submit to it?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For thus it becometh us to fulfil all righteousness, (Matthew 3:15)

### Section 4

**Q:** Is water baptism a sign of our fellowship with Christ?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For as many as have been baptized into Christ have put on Christ, (Galatians 3:27)

**Q:** Is it a sign of our fellowship with him in his death?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** We are baptized into his death, (Romans 6:3)

**Q:** And in his burial?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** We are buried with him in baptism, (Romans 6:4)

**Q:** And in his resurrection?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For we are planted together in the likeness of his resurrection, (Romans 6:5)

**Q:** Is this fellowship by faith?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** We are risen with him through the faith of the operation of God, (Colossians 2:12)

### Section 5

**Q:** Is baptism a sign of our ingrafting into Christ?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** We are baptized into Jesus Christ, (Romans 6:3)

**Q:** And of the crucifixion of the old man?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Knowing this, that our old man is crucified with him, (Romans 6:6)

**Q:** And of the destruction of sin?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** That the body of sin might be destroyed, (Romans 6:6)

**Q:** Doth it signify the removal of the guilt of sin?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Be baptized for the remission of sins, (Acts 2:38)

**Q:** And the washing away the filth of it?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Be baptized and wash away thy sins, (Acts 22:16)

**Q:** But is baptism of itself sufficient for any of these purposes?  
**A:** No.  
**Proof:** For it is the blood of Christ that cleanseth us from all sin, (1 John 1:7)

### Section 6

**Q:** Is baptism an engagement to yield ourselves unto God?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Yield yourselves unto God as those that are alive from the dead, (Romans 6:13)

**Q:** And to live in brotherly love?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For we are baptized into one body, (1 Corinthians 12:13)

**Q:** And to walk in newness of life?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** We also should walk in newness of life, (Romans 6:4)

**Q:** And was it designed to be a standing ordinance in the church of Christ?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** To the end of the world, (Matthew 28:20)
