The Spirit applieth to us the redemption purchased by Christ, by working faith in us, and thereby uniting us to Christ, in our effectual calling.

## Proofs
- The Spirit applieth to us the redemption purchased by Christ, by working faith in us, (Ephesians 1:13–14; John 6:37, 39; Ephesians 2:8)
- and thereby uniting us to Christ, in our effectual calling. (Ephesians 3:17; 1 Corinthians 1:9)

## Additional Exposition

### Section 1

**Q:** Is the application of redemption limited by the purpose of God?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** It shall be given to them for whom it is prepared of my Father, (Matthew 20:23)

**Q:** And by the purchase of Christ?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Ye believe not because ye are not of my sheep, (John 10:26)

**Q:** And by the operations of the Spirit?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For we are saved by the washing of regeneration, and the renewing of the Holy Ghost, (Titus 3:5)

### Section 2

**Q:** Is faith necessary in order to our participation of the blessings of redemption?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** He that believeth not is condemned already, (John 3:18)

**Q:** Is it the great thing necessary?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Only believe, all things are possible to him that believeth, (Mark 5:36)

**Q:** Is it therefore bestowed upon all God’s redeemed ones?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** They have all obtained like precious faith, (2 Peter 1:1)

**Q:** Is the same measure of faith bestowed upon every one?  
**A:** No.  
**Proof:** For unto every one of us is given grace according to the measure of the gift of Christ, (Ephesians 4:7)

**Q:** But does weak faith interest in gospel privileges as well as strong?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For the righteousness of God is unto all, and upon all them that believe, and there is no difference, (Romans 3:22)

### Section 3

**Q:** Are all true believers united to Christ?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** I in them, and thou in me, that they may be made perfect in one, (John 17:23)

**Q:** Are the souls of believers united to Christ?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** He that is joined to the Lord is one spirit, (1 Corinthians 6:17)

**Q:** And their bodies?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Know ye not that your bodies are the members of Christ? (1 Corinthians 6:15)

**Q:** Is this a vital union?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Because I live, ye shall live also, (John 14:19)

**Q:** Is it a lasting and indissolvable union?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** I will betroth thee to me for ever, (Hosea 2:19)

**Q:** But is it an inexplicable and mysterious one?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** This is a great mystery, (Ephesians 5:32)

### Section 4

**Q:** Are we vitally united to Christ by the Spirit?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** We are an habitation of God through the Spirit, (Ephesians 2:22)

**Q:** And that in our effectual calling?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** We are called into the fellowship of his Son Jesus Christ, (1 Corinthians 1:9)

**Q:** But is there an union prior to our effectual calling?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For we read of the grace given us in Christ before the world began, (2 Timothy 1:9)

### Section 5

**Q:** Are all the saints effectually called?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** They are preserved and called, (Jude 1)

**Q:** Is God the author of this call?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** He hath called us out of darkness into his marvellous light, (1 Peter 2:9)

**Q:** Is grace the motive of it?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** It pleased God who called me by his grace, (Galatians 1:15)

**Q:** Is the gospel the means of it?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Whereunto he called you by our gospel, (2 Thessalonians 2:14)

**Q:** Is glory the end of it?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Who hath called you to his kingdom and glory, (1 Thessalonians 2:12)

**Q:** Should we then examine our title to this privilege?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Make your calling and election sure, (2 Peter 1:10)

**Q:** And endeavour to improve it?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Walk worthy of the vocation wherewith ye are called, (Ephesians 4:1)
