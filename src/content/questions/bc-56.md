The second commandment forbiddeth the worshipping of God by images or any other way not appointed in his word.

## Proofs
- The second commandment forbiddeth the worshipping of God by images (Deuteronomy 4:15–19; Exodus 32:5, 8)
- or any other way not appointed in his word. (Deuteronomy 12:31–32)

## Additional Exposition

<a href="https://baptistcatechism.org/beddome/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">From Benjamin Beddome</a>


### Section 1

**Q:** Is it a sin to worship any creature?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** They served the creature more than the Creator, (Romans 1:25)

**Q:** Or the image of any creature?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Cursed be the man that maketh any graven or molten image an abomination unto the Lord, (Deuteronomy 27:15)

**Q:** May we not worship the host of heaven?  
**A:** No.  
**Proof:** Lest when thou seest the sun and the moon and the stars, thou shouldst be driven to worship them, (Deuteronomy 4:19)

**Q:** Nor great and good men?  
**A:** No.  
**Proof:** Why do ye these things, we are also men of like passions with you, (Acts 14:15)

**Q:** Nor good angels?  
**A:** No.  
**Proof:** Let no man beguile you of your reward, by a voluntary humility and worshipping of angels, (Colossians 2:18)

### Section 2

**Q:** Is it a sin to plead for idols?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Will ye plead for Baal? (Judges 6:31)

**Q:** Or pray to them?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** One shall cry to him yet he cannot answer, (Isaiah 46:7)

**Q:** Or trust in them?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** They shall be greatly ashamed that trust in graven images, (Isaiah 42:17)

**Q:** Or attribute any thing to them?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Lest thou shouldst say, My idol hath done them, (Isaiah 48:5)

**Q:** Or give them any part of religious worship?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** If my heart hath been secretly enticed, or my mouth hath kissed my hand this were an iniquity, to be punished by the Judge, (Job 31:27)

### Section 3

**Q:** Is it a sin to worship the true God by images?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Take ye therefore good heed unto yourselves, for ye saw no manner of similitude, (Deuteronomy 4:15–16)

**Q:** Can we form any image of God in our minds?  
**A:** No.  
**Proof:** To whom will ye liken God? (Isaiah 40:18)

**Q:** Is it impossible then to form it with our hands?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For we must not think that the Godhead is like unto gold or silver, or stone graven by art or man’s device, (Acts 17:29)

**Q:** Do those therefore that attempt it put a great affront upon him?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** They change the glory of the incorruptible God into an image made like unto corruptible man, (Romans 1:23)

### Section 4

**Q:** Doth this commandment forbid all superstitious practices in worship?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** In all things ye are too superstitious, (Acts 17:22)

**Q:** And all sinful compliances with the invention of men?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Touch not, taste not, handle not after the commandments and doctrines of men, (Colossians 2:21–22)

**Q:** Does it particularly condemn the idolatry of the papists in worshipping a consecrated host?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For it is bread that we break, (1 Corinthians 10:16)

**Q:** Is it enough that these things are not expressly forbidden?  
**A:** No.  
**Proof:** They offered strange five, which the Lord commanded them not, (Leviticus 10)
