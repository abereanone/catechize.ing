Every sin, even the least, being against the sovereignty, goodness, and holiness of God, and against his righteous law, deserveth his wrath and curse, both in this life, and that which is to come; and cannot be expiated but by the blood of Christ.

## Proofs
- Every sin, even the least, being against the sovereignty, (James 2:10–11)
- goodness, (Exodus 20:1–2)
- and holiness of God, (Habakkuk 1:13; Leviticus 10:3; 11:44–45)
- and against his righteous law, (1 John 3:4; Romans 7:12)
- deserveth his wrath and curse, (Ephesians 5:6; Galatians 3:10)
- both in this life, (Lamentations 3:39; Deuteronomy 28:15–18)
- and that which is to come; (Matthew 25:41)
- and cannot be expiated but by the blood of Christ. (Hebrews 9:22; 1 Peter 1:18–19)
