Christ executeth the office of prophet in revealing to us, by his word and Spirit, the will of God for our salvation.

## Proofs
- Christ executeth the office of prophet in revealing to us, by his word and Spirit, the will of God for our salvation. (John 1:18; 1 Peter 1:10–12; John 15:15; 20:31)

## Additional Exposition

### Section 1

**Q:** Was Christ’s prophetical office foretold under the Old Testament?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** The Lord thy God will raise up unto thee, from the midst of thee, a prophet like unto me, (Deuteronomy 18:15)

**Q:** Was Moses then a great prophet?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** There arose not a prophet since in Israel like unto Moses, (Deuteronomy 34:10)

**Q:** Was Christ like unto Moses raised up from amongst his brethren?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For he was taken from among men, (Hebrews 5:1)

**Q:** Was he like Moses in meekness?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Learn of me, for I am meek, (Matthew 11:29)

**Q:** And in faithfulness?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** All things that I have heard of the Father, I have made known unto you, (John 15:15)

**Q:** Did he like Moses confirm his doctrine by miracles?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** The works that I do, bear witness of me, (John 5:36)

**Q:** But was he greater than Moses?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For Moses was faithful as a servant, but Christ as a Son, (Hebrews 3:5)

### Section 2

**Q:** Did Christ as a prophet foretell future events?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** The testimony of Jesus is the spirit of prophecy, (Revelation 19:10)

**Q:** Did he open the way of salvation by himself?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Grace and truth came by Jesus Christ, (John 1:17)

**Q:** Did he make a fuller discovery of a future state than had been made before?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Life and immortality are brought to light by the gospel, (2 Timothy 1:10)

**Q:** Did he reveal the whole will of God?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** I have given them the words which thou gavest unto me, (John 17:8)

### Section 3

**Q:** Was Christ a divine teacher?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** We know that thou art a teacher come from God, (John 3:2)

**Q:** Was he a plain teacher?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Now speakest thou plainly, (John 16:29)

**Q:** Was he a prudent teacher?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** He taught them as they were able to bear, (Mark 4:33)

**Q:** Was he an affectionate teacher?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** They wondered at the gracious words that proceed out of his mouth, (Luke 4:22)

**Q:** Was he a powerful teacher?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** He taught them as one having authority, (Matthew 7:20)

**Q:** Was he an eloquent teacher?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** He had the tongue of the learned, (Isaiah 50:4)

**Q:** But was his success in teaching equal to his qualifications for it?  
**A:** No.  
**Proof:** All day long I have stretched forth my hands to a disobedient and gainsaying people, (Romans 10:21)

### Section 4

**Q:** Did Christ execute the office of a prophet before his incarnation?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For he went and preached to the spirits in prison, (1 Peter 3:19)

**Q:** Did he execute this office whilst upon earth?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Jesus went about teaching in their synagogues, (Matthew 4:23)

**Q:** And doth he execute it in heaven?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** He speaketh from heaven, (Hebrews 12:25)

**Q:** Doth Christ reveal the will of God objectively by his word?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** These things are written that ye might believe, (John 20:31)

**Q:** And subjectively by his Spirit?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** When the spirit of truth is come, he will guide you into all truth, (John 16:13)

**Q:** And is the latter as well as the former necessary to our salvation?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For if any man have not the Spirit of Christ he is none of his, (Romans 8:9)

### Practical response

**Q:** Should we learn of this teacher?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Hear ye him, (Matthew 17:5)

**Q:** And is it at our peril if we do not?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Every soul that will not hear that Prophet shall be destroyed from among the people, (Acts 3:23)
