Christ executeth the office of a king, in subduing us to himself, in ruling, and defending us, and in restraining and conquering all his and our enemies.

## Proofs
- Christ executeth the office of a king, in subduing us to himself, (Acts 15:14–16)
- in ruling, (Isaiah 33:22)
- and defending us, (Isaiah 32:1–2)
- and in restraining and conquering all his and our enemies. (1 Corinthians 15:25; Psalm 110 throughout)

## Additional Exposition

### Section 1

**Q:** Is Christ a King?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** There is another King, one Jesus, (Acts 17:7)

**Q:** Doth Christ execute the kingly office?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** The government shall be upon his shoulder, (Isaiah 9:6)

**Q:** And is he duly qualified so to do?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** He is the Lamb with seven horns, and seven eyes, (Revelation 5:6)

**Q:** Is he King as Mediator?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** He hath authority to execute judgment, because he is the Son of Man, (John 5:27)

**Q:** And is his mediatorial government founded upon the performance of his mediatorial engagements?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Because he humbled himself and became obedient unto death, even the death of the cross, therefore God hath highly exalted him, (Philippians 2:8)

### Section 2

**Q:** Is Christ a powerful king?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** All power is given to him both in heaven and earth, (Matthew 28:18)

**Q:** Is he a merciful king?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** He is meek and having salvation, (Zechariah 9:9)

**Q:** Is he a universal king?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** He is Governor among the nations, (Psalm 22:28)

**Q:** Is he a righteous king?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** A king shall reign in righteousness, (Isaiah 32:1)

**Q:** And is he a rightful one?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Unto him was given dominion and glory and a kingdom, (Daniel 7:14)

**Q:** Should we then submit to his authority?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Take my yoke upon you, (Matthew 11:29)

### Section 3

**Q:** Is Christ in an especial manner the King of saints?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Just and true are thy ways, thou King of saints, (Revelation 15:3)

**Q:** Are such naturally the subjects of another kingdom?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Other Lords besides thee have had the dominion over us, (Isaiah 26:13)

**Q:** But are they in due time rescued and delivered?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Even the captives of the mighty shall be taken away, and the prey of the terrible shall be delivered, (Isaiah 49:25)

**Q:** And doth Christ govern those whom he hath thus subdued?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For out of thee shall come a governor that shall rule my people Israel, (Matthew 2:6)

### Section 4

**Q:** Is Christ king over the church?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Is not the Lord in Zion, is not the King in her, (Jeremiah 8:19)

**Q:** Doth he enact laws there?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Teaching them to observe all things whatsoever I have commanded you, (Matthew 28:20)

**Q:** Doth he commission officers?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** He gave some apostles and some prophets, (Ephesians 4:11)

**Q:** And does he resent the introduction of any thing without his authority?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** In their setting their threshold by my threshold, and their post by my post, they have defiled my holy name, (Ezekiel 43:8)

### Section 5

**Q:** Doth Christ as a King preserve his subjects?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** We are preserved in Christ Jesus, (Jude 1)

**Q:** And protect them?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** As birds flying so he defends Jerusalem, (Isaiah 31:5)

**Q:** And punish their faults?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Whom he loveth he chasteneth, (Hebrews 12:6)

**Q:** And reward their faithful services?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** His reward is with him, (Revelation 22:12)

### Section 6

**Q:** Doth Christ exercise his kingly power in restraining his enemies?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** I will put my hook in thy nose, and my bridle in thy lips, (Isaiah 37:29)

**Q:** And in subduing them?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** He hath spoiled principalities and powers, (Colossians 2:15)

**Q:** And in destroying them?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** He will break them with a rod of iron, and dash them in pieces like a potter’s vessel, (Psalm 2:9)

**Q:** Will the conquest of the saints’ enemies be gradual?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** I will drive them out by little and little, (Exodus 23:30)

**Q:** But will it be total?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For he must reign till he hath put all enemies under his feet, (1 Corinthians 15:25)
