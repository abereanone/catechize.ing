The decrees of God are his eternal purpose according to the counsel of his will, whereby, for his own glory, he hath foreordained whatsoever comes to pass.

## Proofs
- The decrees of God are his eternal purpose according to the counsel of his will, whereby, for his own glory, he hath foreordained whatsoever comes to pass. (Ephesians 1:4, 11; Romans 9:22–23; Isaiah 46:10; Lamentations 3:37)

## Additional Exposition

### Section 1

**Q:** Hath God pre-determined all future events?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** He worketh all things according to the counsel of his own will, (Ephesians 1:11)

**Q:** Doth nothing then come by chance?  
**A:** No.  
**Proof:** The lot is cast into the lap, but the whole disposal thereof is of the Lord, (Proverbs 16:33)

**Q:** Do God’s decrees extend to the conduct of good men?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** They are created unto good works, which God hath before ordained that they should walk in them, (Ephesians 2:19)

**Q:** And to their salvation?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** They are vessels of mercy, which he hath afore prepared to glory, (Romans 9:23)

**Q:** Do God’s decrees extend to the conduct of wicked men?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Him being delivered by the determinate counsel of God, ye by wicked hands have crucified and slain, (Acts 2:23)

**Q:** And to their destruction?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Who were of old ordained to this condemnation, (Jude 4)

**Q:** Is it lawful for men to cavil at God’s decrees?  
**A:** No.  
**Proof:** Who art thou that repliest against God? Hath not the potter power over the clay, of the same lump, to make one vessel to honour, and another unto dishonour? (Romans 9:20)

### Section 2

**Q:** Are God’s decrees sovereign and free?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** They are according to the good pleasure of his will, (Ephesians 1:5)

**Q:** Are they eternal?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For we are chosen in Christ before the foundation of the world, (Ephesians 1:4)

**Q:** Are they infinitely wise?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** We read of the manifold wisdom of God according to his eternal purpose, (Ephesians 3:10–11)

**Q:** Are they most holy?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Thy counsels of old are faithfulness and truth, (Isaiah 25:1)

**Q:** Are they impenetrable and unsearchable?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Who hath known the mind of the Lord? (Romans 11:34)

**Q:** Are they absolute and unchangeable?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** He is in one mind, and who can turn him? (Job 23:13)

**Q:** Are they all calculated for his own glory?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** He hath made all things for himself, (Proverbs 16:4)

### Section 3

**Q:** Are all God’s decrees accomplished?  
**A:** No.  
**Proof:** He declares from ancient times the things that are not yet done, (Isaiah 46:10)

**Q:** But will they be all accomplished?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Surely as I have thought, so shall it come to pass, (Isaiah 14:24)

**Q:** And will any thing come to pass that God hath not decreed?  
**A:** No.  
**Proof:** For who is he that saith, and it cometh to pass, when the Lord commandeth it not? (Lamentations 3:37)

### Practical response

**Q:** Should we be curious to know the divine decrees?  
**A:** No.  
**Proof:** For secret things belong to God, (Deuteronomy 29:29)

**Q:** Are God’s precepts then, and not his decrees, the rule of our conduct?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Things which are revealed belong unto us and to our children, (Deuteronomy 29:29)
