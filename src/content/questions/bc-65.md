The Sabbath is to be sanctified by a holy resting all that day, even from such worldly employments and recreations as are lawful on other days; and spending the whole time in the public and private exercises of God’s worship, except so much as is to be taken up in the works of necessity and mercy.

## Proofs
- The Sabbath is to be sanctified by a holy resting all that day, (Exodus 20:8, 10)
- even from such worldly employments and recreations as are lawful on other days; (Exodus 16:25–28; Nehemiah 13:15–22)
- and spending the whole time in the public and private exercises of God’s worship, (Luke 4:16; Acts 20:7; Psalm 92:title; Isaiah 66:23)
- except so much as is to be taken up in the works of necessity and mercy. (Matthew 12:1–13)

## Additional Exposition

### Section 1

**Q:** Must we sanctify the sabbath?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Keep the sabbath to sanctify it, (Deuteronomy 5:12)

**Q:** Must the whole sabbath be sanctified?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** When the gates of Jerusalem began to be dark, before the sabbath I commanded that they should be shut, and that they should not be opened till after the sabbath, (Nehemiah 13:19)

### Section 2

**Q:** Must we keep the sabbath as a day of rest?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** It is a sabbath of rest, (Leviticus 23:3)

**Q:** And do no work thereon?  
**A:** No.  
**Proof:** Ye shall do no work therein, (Leviticus 23:3)

**Q:** Must we avoid those labours which are permitted on other days?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Take heed on yourselves and bear no burden on the sabbath day, (Jeremiah 17:21)

**Q:** And which are required on other days?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Six days shall ye gather manna, but on the seventh which is the sabbath, there shall be none, (Exodus 16:26)

**Q:** And does this extend to most busy seasons of the year?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For in earing time, and in harvest thou shalt rest, (Exodus 34:21)

**Q:** Is buying and selling therefore unlawful on the sabbath-day?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** I testified against them in the day that they sold victuals, (Nehemiah 13:15)

### Practical response

**Q:** Should public worship be maintained on the sabbath-day?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** It is an holy convocation, (Leviticus 23:3)

**Q:** And should we make conscience of attending upon it?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Not forsaking the assembling yourselves together, (Hebrews 10:25)

**Q:** And that early?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Early will I seek thee, (Psalm 63)

**Q:** But should we confine our religious services to the house of God?  
**A:** No.  
**Proof:** It is the sabbath of the Lord in all our dwellings, (Leviticus 23:3)

### Section 4

**Q:** Is prayer a part of sabbath service?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** On the sabbath-day we went to the river’s side, where prayer was wont to be made, (Acts 16:13)

**Q:** And singing of psalms?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** We have a psalm or song for the sabbath-day, (Psalm 92)

**Q:** And hearing?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** The next sabbath day came almost the whole city together to hear the word, (Acts 13:44)

**Q:** And Christian conversation?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** They talked together of all those things that had happened, (Luke 24:14)

### Practical response

**Q:** Should we keep the sabbath with reverence?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Keep thy foot when thou goest into the house of God, (Ecclesiastes 5:1)

**Q:** And with diligence?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** We must lay hold on the sabbath to keep it from polluting it, (Isaiah 56:2)

**Q:** And must we honour God in all the duties of it?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** We must call the sabbath honorable and honor him, (Isaiah 58:13)

**Q:** Are men apt to watch how we keep the sabbath?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** As he went into the house of one of the chief Pharisees on the sabbath day they watched him, (Luke 14:1)

**Q:** And should this make us circumspect?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Lead me in a plain path because of mine enemies, (marg. observers,) (Psalm 27:11)

### Section 6

**Q:** Are works of charity and mercy to be done on the sabbath day?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Ought not this woman to be loosed from this bond on the sabbath day? (Luke 13:16)

**Q:** And works of necessity?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Jesus went on the sabbath through the corn and his disciples were an hungered and began to pluck the ears of corn to eat, (Matthew 12:1)

**Q:** Are Christians obliged to the same strictness in keeping the sabbath as the Jews?  
**A:** No.  
**Proof:** Ye have been called unto liberty, (Galatians 5:13)

**Q:** But should they be careful not to abuse their liberty?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Use not your liberty for an occasion to the flesh, (Galatians 5:13)

**Q:** And is conscience as directed by the word the only judge in this case?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Happy is he that condemneth not himself in that which he alloweth, (Romans 14:22)
