The outward and ordinary means whereby Christ communicateth to us the benefits of redemption are his ordinances, especially the word, baptism, the Lord’s supper, and prayer; all which means are made effectual to the elect for salvation.

## Proofs
- The outward and ordinary means whereby Christ communicateth to us the benefits of redemption are his ordinances, especially the word, baptism, the Lord’s supper, and prayer; all which means are made effectual to the elect for salvation. (Matthew 28:19–20; Acts 2:42, 46–47)

## Additional Exposition

<a href="https://baptistcatechism.org/beddome/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">From Benjamin Beddome</a>


### Section 1

**Q:** Are there ordinances which are not of God’s appointment?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** We read of the statutes of Omri, (Micah 6:16)

**Q:** But are these unprofitable?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For those that observe lying vanities, forsake their own mercies, (Jonah 2:8)

**Q:** And should they therefore be rejected?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Why are we subject to ordinances after the commandments of men? (Colossians 2:20)

### Section 2

**Q:** Are there ordinances of God’s appointment?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Thou shalt teach them my ordinances, (Exodus 18:20)

**Q:** Are the word, sacraments and prayer the great gospel ordinances?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Then they that gladly received the word were baptized, and they continued stedfastly in the apostles’ doctrine and fellowship, and in breaking of bread, and in prayers, (Acts 2:41–42)

**Q:** Is singing of psalms also an ordinance?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** O come, let us sing unto the Lord, let us make a joyful noise to the Rock of our salvation, (Psalm 95:1)

**Q:** Is it an ordinance very pleasing to God?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** This also shall please the Lord better than an ox or a bullock, (Psalm 69:31)

**Q:** And very useful and instructive to men?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Teaching and admonishing one another in psalms and hymns and spiritual (Colossians 3:16)

**Q:** Should it therefore be attended to in a very serious manner?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Singing with grace in your hearts unto the Lord, (Colossians 3:16)

**Q:** Are these ordinances made effectual to all God’s elect?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** For as many as were ordained to eternal life believed, (Acts 13:48)

**Q:** And to none but such?  
**A:** No.  
**Proof:** Ye believe not because ye are not my sheep, (John 10:26)

### Section 3

**Q:** Have some been wrought upon by religious discourse?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** He that walketh with wise men shall be wise, (Proverbs 13:20)

**Q:** And others by dreams?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** In a dream, in a vision of the night, he openeth the ears of men and sealeth their instruction, (Job 33:15–16)

**Q:** And others by afflictive providence?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** When Manasseh was in affliction he humbled himself before the God of his fathers, (2 Chronicles 33:12)

**Q:** And others in a yet more miraculous manner?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Thus Saul was called by a voice from heaven, (Acts 9:4)

**Q:** But are God’s word and ordinances the ordinary means of salvation?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** He hath given pastors and teachers for the edifying of the body of Christ, (Ephesians 4:11–12)

**Q:** Should we therefore attend upon them?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Blessed is the man that heareth me, (Proverbs 8:34)

**Q:** And that diligently and constantly?  
**A:** Yes.  
**Proof:** Watching daily at my gates, and waiting at the post of my doors, (Proverbs 8:34)
